# Growth Auditor Output Template

## Purpose

This template defines the structured output the Growth Auditor produces when conducting a
coherence audit across marketing team outputs. The audit synthesizes individual team member
contributions into a unified growth assessment, identifies conflicts and gaps, and produces
a prioritized action plan ranked by compounding potential.

---

## Pre-Launch Venture Guidance

For pre-launch ventures: Focus on foundation-building over optimization. Use projected metrics labeled [PROJECTED]. Prioritize organic/low-budget channels. Note where live data is needed before scaling.

## When to Use This Template

Produce this output when:
- Reviewing outputs from multiple marketing team members (strategist, copywriter, etc.)
- Conducting a periodic growth strategy review
- Diagnosing why growth has stalled or decelerated
- Planning resource allocation for the next quarter
- Onboarding stakeholders to the current growth posture

### Section 8: Prioritized Action Plan

**Purpose:** Rank all recommended actions by compounding potential. Actions that improve the
growth engine or its foundation (retention) rank higher than one-time optimizations or boosts.

```
## Prioritized Action Plan

### Prioritization Criteria
Actions are ranked by:
1. **Compounding potential:** Does this action improve a loop or one-time metric?
2. **Impact magnitude:** How large is the expected effect?
3. **Time to impact:** How quickly will results materialize?
4. **Resource requirement:** What investment is needed?
5. **Confidence level:** How certain are we this will work?

### Impact Scoring System
- Compounding Potential: 1 (one-time) to 5 (high compounding)
- Impact Magnitude: 1 (minor) to 5 (transformative)
- Speed: 1 (12+ months) to 5 (< 1 month)
- Efficiency: 1 (heavy resources) to 5 (minimal resources)
- Confidence: 1 (speculative) to 5 (proven)
- **Composite Score = (Compounding x 2) + Impact + Speed + Efficiency + Confidence**
  (Compounding is double-weighted because it is the most important factor for long-term growth)

### Action Plan

| Priority | Action | Component | Compound | Impact | Speed | Efficiency | Confidence | Score | Owner |
|----------|--------|-----------|----------|--------|-------|------------|------------|-------|-------|
```

## Output Quality Checklist

Before finalizing the output, verify:

- [ ] All sections are completed with specific data, not generic placeholders
- [ ] Conflicts identified in Section 1 are addressed in the Action Plan (Section 8)
- [ ] The Growth Loop Map (Section 2) is consistent with the Racecar Diagnostic (Section 3)
- [ ] Retention Risk Assessment (Section 4) is reflected in the Unified Growth Model (Section 7)
- [ ] Channel-Model Fit (Section 5) aligns with the loop types identified (Section 2)
- [ ] Ceiling Analysis (Section 6) informs the sequencing in the Action Plan (Section 8)
- [ ] The Unified Growth Model (Section 7) tells a coherent story that synthesizes all sections
- [ ] Action Plan priorities weight compounding actions over one-time actions
- [ ] Every recommendation is specific enough to be actionable
- [ ] The output identifies the single most important thing to do next

---

## Usage Notes

- This template is a maximum structure. Not every section will be equally relevant for every
  audit. Use judgment about depth -- go deep where the diagnosis reveals problems and lighter
  where things are healthy.
- The Coherence Score (Section 1) and Retention Foundation Score (Section 4) should be tracked
  over time to monitor improvement.
- The Prioritized Action Plan should be updated after each audit cycle, with completed actions
  removed and new ones added based on fresh analysis.
- Share the Unified Growth Model narrative with stakeholders as the executive summary. The
  detailed sections serve as supporting evidence.
