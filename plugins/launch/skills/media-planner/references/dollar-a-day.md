## The Core Principle: Never Scale What You Haven't Tested at $1/Day

Dollar-a-Day is a **risk-reduction system**, not a budget constraint. The philosophy:

1. Every ad, every audience, every creative angle starts at $1/day.
2. $1/day for 7 days = $7 total — the minimum viable test.
3. At $7, you get enough impressions and engagement data to determine signal.
4. Only content that proves it resonates gets more budget.
5. Everything else gets killed — and 90% of tests should be killed.

This is the paid media equivalent of a scientific method: **hypothesis → controlled
experiment → measure → decide.** The $1/day budget ensures every experiment is cheap
enough that failure costs almost nothing, so you can run many experiments simultaneously.

### Why Specifically $1/Day

| Reason                    | Explanation                                              |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Platform minimum          | $1/day is the minimum most platforms allow (Meta, TikTok)|
| Statistical signal        | 7 days at $1 generates 500-2,000+ impressions — enough  |
|                           | to measure engagement rate with reasonable confidence    |
| Psychological safety      | Teams and clients test more aggressively when each test  |
|                           | costs only $7/week — removes the "what if it fails" fear |
| Parallel testing          | Run 20 tests simultaneously for $20/day ($140/week) —   |
|                           | this is portfolio-style experimentation                  |
| Platform agnostic         | Works on Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, Google, YouTube, X,     |
|                           | Pinterest — any platform with a minimum spend threshold  |
| Eliminates guessing       | Instead of debating which creative or audience will work,|
|                           | let real data from micro-tests decide                    |

### Platform-Specific Minimum Budgets

Dollar-a-Day is a Meta-native strategy. For other platforms, scale the minimum budget accordingly while maintaining the signal-testing philosophy:

| Platform | Minimum Daily Budget | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | $1/day | Works as designed — the original Dollar-a-Day platform |
| LinkedIn | $10/day | Adjust Dollar-a-Day to "Ten-a-Day" for LinkedIn campaigns |
| Google Ads | $5/day | Recommended minimum for meaningful signal |
| TikTok | $5/day | Platform minimum for ad delivery |

### What Dollar-a-Day Is NOT

- **Not a budget strategy.** The goal is not to spend only $1/day forever. The goal is to
  find the 10% of content that deserves real budget and scale it aggressively.
- **Not a shortcut.** Dollar-a-Day requires rigorous daily monitoring, ruthless killing of
  underperformers, and continuous creation of new test content.
- **Not for lazy marketers.** The system demands high volume of creative input, disciplined
  measurement, and willingness to kill content you personally like but the data says fails.

## The Testing Hierarchy: Content First, Then Audience, Then Placement

Dennis Yu enforces a strict testing order. Do not skip levels.

```
TESTING HIERARCHY (Top → Bottom)

Level 1: CONTENT (Test first)
  → Does this content resonate with ANYONE?
  → Test 10+ pieces of content against your warmest audience
  → Metric: 10%+ engagement rate
  → Kill 90%, keep the unicorns

Level 2: AUDIENCE (Test second)
  → Does the winning content resonate with NEW audiences?
  → Take proven content, test against 5-10 different audience segments
  → Metric: Engagement rate holds above 8% with new audience
  → Identify which audience segments respond best

Level 3: PLACEMENT (Test third)
  → Where does the winning content + audience perform best?
  → Test across Feed, Stories, Reels, Search, Audience Network
  → Metric: Cost efficiency (CPC, CPM, CPA) by placement
  → Allocate budget to winning placements

Level 4: OPTIMIZATION (Ongoing)
  → Bid strategy, scheduling, frequency management
  → Only worth optimizing once Levels 1-3 are proven
```

**Why this order matters:** Testing placement before you have proven content is pointless —
bad content performs badly everywhere. Testing audiences before content is wasteful — you
do not know if the content or the audience caused the failure. Content is the independent
variable you control most directly.

## The Boosting Organic Winners Strategy

The Dollar-a-Day approach begins with organic content, not paid-first creative. This is
the foundation that separates Dollar-a-Day from conventional media buying:

### Step 1: Publish Content Organically First
- Post content across owned channels: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube.
- Aim for consistent volume: minimum 3-5 posts per week.
- Include a mix of formats: video (prioritized), images, carousels, text posts.
- Do not create "ads." Create content people would engage with even without paid behind it.

### Step 2: Identify What Resonates Naturally
- After 48-72 hours, review engagement metrics on each post.
- Compare each post's engagement rate to your baseline average.
- Look for posts that significantly outperform (2x+ your average engagement rate).
- Signals of organic winners:
  - Higher-than-average comments (especially substantive ones, not just emojis)
  - Shares and saves (high-intent actions)
  - Above-average watch time on video (especially 15+ second retention)
  - DMs or inbound inquiries triggered by the post

### Step 3: Boost Winners with $1/Day
- Take the top 3-5 organic performers and put $1/day behind each.
- Run each boost for 7 days — this is the standard test window.
- Keep the original organic post as the ad (do not create a new "dark post") so that
  social proof (existing likes, comments, shares) carries over.
- Target your existing fans/followers plus a 1% lookalike initially.

### Step 4: Kill Underperformers
- After 7 days, evaluate each boosted post against the 10% engagement rate benchmark.
- Dennis Yu's rule: **expect to kill 90% of boosted posts.** This is not failure — this is
  the system working. You are filtering for signal.
- Posts below 10% engagement after 7 days get spend stopped immediately.
- Do not give underperformers "more time" or "more budget" hoping they improve — they will not.

### Step 5: Identify Unicorn Posts
- The rare posts (roughly 1 in 10) that achieve 10%+ engagement rate are unicorns.
- These are your scale candidates — the content that the audience has voted deserves more reach.
- Unicorn characteristics:
  - 10%+ engagement rate sustained over 7 days
  - Positive comment sentiment (not controversy-driven engagement)
  - Strong video completion rates (if video)
  - Low cost per engagement/ThruPlay

### Why Organic First Works

```
ORGANIC-FIRST vs. AD-FIRST APPROACH

Organic-First (Dollar-a-Day):             Ad-First (Conventional):
  Publish 20 posts organically              Create 5 ads in a design tool
        ↓                                          ↓
  3-5 posts show organic traction           Launch all 5 with $100/day each
        ↓                                          ↓
  Boost those 3-5 at $1/day                 Wait 7 days, most fail
        ↓                                          ↓
  1 unicorn emerges (10%+ engagement)       Spent $3,500 to find 1 winner
        ↓                                          ↓
  Scale unicorn with real budget            Could have found it for $35
        ↓                                          ↓
  Total test cost: $35                      Total test cost: $3,500
  Risk: minimal                             Risk: significant
  Speed: same (7 days)                      Speed: same (7 days)
```

## The 10% Engagement Rate Benchmark

The key performance signal in the Dollar-a-Day framework:

### Definition
- **Engagement Rate** = (Total Engagements / Reach) x 100
- Engagements include: reactions, comments, shares, saves, clicks, video views (ThruPlay)
- Measured at the **ad level**, not campaign or ad set level

### Thresholds

| Engagement Rate | Signal         | Action                                        |
|-----------------|----------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| 15%+            | Unicorn        | Scale immediately — this is rare gold          |
| 10-15%          | Strong winner  | Scale gradually — increase budget 2x per step  |
| 7-10%           | Promising      | Keep running, test with new audiences          |
| 5-7%            | Weak signal    | Monitor for 3 more days, then likely kill      |
| 3-5%            | Poor           | Kill after 7 days — not worth budget           |
| <3%             | Dead           | Kill immediately — do not wait 7 days          |

### Platform-Specific Engagement Actions

| Platform   | Primary Engagement Actions                                      |
|------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Meta/FB    | Reactions, comments, shares, saves, link clicks, ThruPlay       |
| Instagram  | Likes, comments, shares, saves, profile visits, story replies   |
| TikTok     | Likes, comments, shares, saves, profile visits, follows         |
| LinkedIn   | Reactions, comments, shares, clicks, follows                    |
| YouTube    | Likes, comments, shares, watch time, subscribes                 |

### Why 10% Specifically

Dennis Yu arrived at the 10% benchmark through analysis of billions of dollars in spend:
- Below 10%, the content is not resonating strongly enough to justify scaling.
- At 10%+, the algorithm rewards the content with lower costs and broader distribution.
- 10%+ indicates genuine audience interest, not just passive exposure.
- The benchmark creates a binary decision: keep or kill. No ambiguity.

## How to Set Up $1/Day Tests (Step-by-Step)

### Pre-Test Plumbing Checklist

Before running any $1/day test, verify:

```
PLUMBING VERIFICATION CHECKLIST

[ ] Platform pixel installed and firing correctly on all pages
[ ] Custom conversion events defined (purchase, lead, add-to-cart, etc.)
[ ] UTM parameter convention established and documented
[ ] Custom audiences configured:
    [ ] Website visitors (30-day, 7-day windows)
    [ ] Video viewers (3s, 15s, ThruPlay)
    [ ] Page/profile engagers (30-day)
    [ ] Customer list uploaded (if available)
[ ] Lookalike audiences created from best customer data
[ ] Google Analytics 4 connected with proper event tracking
[ ] Payment method active and verified on ad account
[ ] Ad account not restricted or flagged
[ ] Business Manager access properly configured
```

**Dennis Yu's rule: Never spend a dollar before plumbing is verified.** The most common
reason Dollar-a-Day "doesn't work" is broken tracking — you cannot measure what you
cannot see.

### Test Setup Procedure

**Step 1: Select Content for Testing**
- Choose 5-10 organic posts that showed above-average engagement.
- If no organic data exists, create 5-10 pieces of content using the 3x3 Grid
  framework (see three-by-three-grid.md) and test those.
- Ensure variety: mix videos (prioritized), images, and carousels.

**Step 2: Create Campaign Structure**
- Campaign objective: Engagement (for initial content testing).
  - Do NOT use Conversion objective for $1/day tests — the algorithm needs volume
    to optimize, and $1/day does not generate enough conversion events.
- One campaign for all tests (keeps reporting clean).
- One ad set per test (isolates variables).
- One ad per ad set (clean data, no algorithm-driven creative selection yet).

**Step 3: Configure Ad Sets**
- Budget: $1/day per ad set.
- Duration: 7 days (set end date so you do not forget to review).
- Audience: Start with your warmest audience (existing fans, website visitors, email list).
  - This tests content first, not audience. Warm audiences give the most honest signal
    about whether content resonates.
- Placements: Automatic (let the platform optimize initially — placement testing comes later).
- Scheduling: Run 24/7 (do not restrict scheduling at $1/day — let the algorithm find
  optimal times).

**Step 4: Launch and Monitor**
- Launch all tests simultaneously.
- Check metrics daily at the same time (build the MAA habit).
- Do NOT make changes during the first 3 days — let the algorithm learn.
- After Day 3: Kill any tests with <3% engagement rate (obvious losers).
- After Day 7: Apply the 10% engagement rate threshold to remaining tests.

**Step 5: Document Results**
- For each test, record:
  - Content description
  - Engagement rate
  - Cost per engagement
  - Cost per ThruPlay (if video)
  - Comment sentiment (positive, negative, neutral)
  - Audience reached vs. audience available
  - Decision: Kill / Keep / Scale

## Graduation Criteria: When to Scale from $1 to $5 to $30 to $100+/Day

Scaling is not a single decision — it is a series of graduated checkpoints. Each level
has specific criteria that must be met before advancing.

### Graduation Level 1: $1/Day → $5/Day

**Criteria (ALL must be met):**
1. Engagement rate >= 10% sustained over 7 days
2. Cost per ThruPlay (if video) < $0.05
3. Comment sentiment is positive (not controversy-driven engagement)
4. No significant metric volatility (steady performance, not spike-driven)

**Action:** Increase budget from $1/day to $5/day. Run for 7 additional days.
**What to watch:** Does engagement rate hold at 5x the budget? A 20%+ drop in
engagement rate at $5/day means the content may not sustain scale.

### Graduation Level 2: $5/Day → $30/Day

**Criteria (ALL must be met):**
1. Engagement rate remains >= 8% at $5/day (slight drop is acceptable)
2. Cost per result is within target CPA/CPL (if running conversion objective)
3. Audience size supports the increase (you are reaching <10% of available audience)
4. Downstream metrics confirm quality: landing page conversion rate, lead quality,
   or purchase rate are acceptable

**Action:** Increase budget to $30/day. Run for 30 days (one full reporting cycle).
**What to watch:** Frequency (keep below 3x/week), audience saturation, engagement
rate decay over time, cost per result trends.

### Graduation Level 3: $30/Day → $100+/Day

**Criteria (ALL must be met):**
1. Performance has been stable at $30/day for 14+ days
2. ROAS is positive and within target (or CPA is within target for lead gen)
3. Frequency is below 3x/week (audience is not oversaturated)
4. Funnel integrity confirmed: ad traffic converts at acceptable rates downstream
5. Audience pool is large enough to support $100+/day without rapid saturation

**Action:** Increase budget by 20-30% increments (not 3x jumps).
  - $30 → $40 → $50 → $65 → $85 → $100
  - Wait 3-5 days between each increment for the algorithm to stabilize.
**What to watch:** Marginal ROAS (is the next dollar spent producing proportional
returns?), creative fatigue signals, audience exhaustion.

### The Scaling Staircase (Visual)

```
BUDGET SCALING STAIRCASE

$100+/day ─────────────── Only if ROAS holds, audience supports it
   ↑   Wait 3-5 days between each 20-30% increase
$30/day ──────────────── 30-day validation at this level
   ↑   Engagement holds >= 8%, downstream metrics confirmed
$5/day ───────────────── 7-day validation at this level
   ↑   Engagement >= 10% for 7 days, positive sentiment
$1/day ───────────────── 7-day initial test
   ↑   Selected from organic winners or 3x3 Grid content

                          90% killed ← ← ← ← ← ← ← ← at each level
```

### Budget Increase Rules

- **Never increase budget by more than 30% at a time.** Larger jumps reset the
  algorithm's learning and cause performance instability.
- **Wait 3-5 days after each increase** before evaluating. The algorithm needs time
  to adjust delivery to the new budget level.
- **If engagement rate drops below 7% after an increase**, pull back to the previous
  budget level immediately. Do not wait to see if it recovers.
- **If CPA increases by more than 25% after scaling**, pause and investigate before
  continuing. The issue is usually audience saturation or creative fatigue.
- **Continue running new $1/day tests in parallel** — never stop the testing pipeline,
  even while scaling winners. Winning ads fatigue eventually; you need the next winner
  ready in the pipeline.

## The 3-Phase Approach: Awareness, Engagement, Conversion

Dollar-a-Day operates within a three-phase funnel. Each phase has different objectives,
metrics, and budget allocation:

### Phase 1: Awareness
- **Objective:** Get seen. Build initial reach and introduce the brand to cold audiences.
- **Content type:** WHY videos — origin stories, mission, values (see 3x3 Grid reference).
- **Campaign objective:** Video Views / ThruPlay.
- **Key metric:** Cost per ThruPlay (target: <$0.05).
- **Budget:** This is where most $1/day tests start.
- **Outcome:** A growing pool of people who have seen your content and can be retargeted.

### Phase 2: Engagement
- **Objective:** Build relationship. Deepen connection with people who showed initial interest.
- **Content type:** HOW videos — tutorials, expertise, teaching (see 3x3 Grid reference).
- **Campaign objective:** Engagement / Traffic.
- **Key metric:** Engagement rate (target: 10%+), click-through rate (target: >2%).
- **Budget:** Serve to warm audiences built from Phase 1 video viewers (15s+).
- **Outcome:** A highly engaged warm audience ready for conversion messaging.

### Phase 3: Conversion
- **Objective:** Drive action. Get leads, sales, sign-ups, bookings.
- **Content type:** WHAT videos — offers, demos, testimonials, CTAs (see 3x3 Grid reference).
- **Campaign objective:** Conversions / Lead Generation.
- **Key metric:** Cost per conversion, ROAS.
- **Budget:** Serve to hot audiences built from Phase 2 engagers.
- **Outcome:** Revenue, leads, measurable business results.

### Phase Budget Distribution

```
PHASE BUDGET DISTRIBUTION

For a $3,000/month total paid budget:

Phase 1 (Awareness):   50% = $1,500/month
  → Feeds the top of funnel, builds remarketing pools
  → 15-30 $1/day tests + scaled awareness winners

Phase 2 (Engagement):  30% = $900/month
  → Deepens relationship with warm audiences
  → 5-10 $1/day tests + scaled engagement winners

Phase 3 (Conversion):  20% = $600/month
  → Converts hot audiences (smallest pool, highest value)
  → 3-5 $1/day tests + scaled conversion winners

Key: More budget goes to top of funnel because:
  - Cold audiences are largest, need most reach investment
  - Without Phase 1 feeding the funnel, Phases 2-3 starve
  - Warm/hot audiences are smaller but higher-converting
```

## Metrics Trees: Connecting Vanity Metrics to Business Outcomes

Dennis Yu's Metrics Tree is a diagnostic framework that connects three levels of metrics
in a cause-and-effect hierarchy:

```
METRICS TREE STRUCTURE

LEVEL 1: BUSINESS GOALS (Lagging — Monthly Review)
├── Revenue
├── Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
├── Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
├── Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
└── Profit Margin

        ↑ caused by ↑

LEVEL 2: INTERMEDIATE METRICS (Middle — Weekly Review)
├── Number of Conversions (leads, sales, sign-ups)
├── Cost per Conversion
├── Conversion Rate (landing page, checkout, form)
├── Average Order Value
├── Lead-to-Customer Rate
├── Warm Audience Pool Size
└── Hot Audience Pool Size

        ↑ caused by ↑

LEVEL 3: LEADING INDICATORS (Leading — Daily Review / MAA Cycle)
├── Engagement Rate (the 10% benchmark)
├── Cost per Click (CPC)
├── Click-Through Rate (CTR)
├── Video View Completion Rates (3s, 15s, ThruPlay)
├── Cost per ThruPlay
├── Relevance Score / Quality Ranking
├── Frequency (ad fatigue indicator)
└── Audience Saturation Percentage
```

### Diagnostic Logic

The Metrics Tree is not just a reporting framework — it is a diagnostic tool:

```
METRICS TREE DIAGNOSTIC FLOWCHART

Level 3 indicators HEALTHY + Level 2 HEALTHY + Level 1 HEALTHY
  → Everything is working. Scale carefully.

Level 3 indicators HEALTHY + Level 2 POOR
  → Ads are performing well, but conversions are not following.
  → DIAGNOSIS: The problem is in the funnel, not the ads.
  → ACTION: Review landing page, offer, form, checkout process.
  → TRIGGER: Send diagnostic to Funnel Architect.

Level 3 indicators POOR
  → Ads themselves are not performing.
  → DIAGNOSIS: Creative or targeting problem.
  → ACTION: Test new creative, test new audiences, review ad relevance.
  → TRIGGER: Request new content from Copywriter, new angles from Offer Designer.

Level 2 indicators HEALTHY + Level 1 POOR
  → Conversions happening, but business metrics are off.
  → DIAGNOSIS: Pricing, LTV, or retention problem.
  → ACTION: Review unit economics, customer quality, pricing structure.
  → TRIGGER: Send signal to Offer Designer — the offer economics are broken.
```

### How an AI Agent Uses the Metrics Tree

When given campaign performance data, the AI agent system should:

1. **Ingest data at all three levels** — do not analyze ad metrics in isolation.
2. **Compute the causal chain** — are Level 3 improvements translating to Level 2 improvements?
3. **Identify the bottleneck level** — where in the tree does performance break down?
4. **Generate specific actions** — not "improve your ads" but "Creative X has 4% engagement
   after 7 days, kill it; Creative Y has 12%, scale to $5/day; Landing page converts at 1.2%
   against a 3% target, flag for Funnel Architect review."
5. **Track trends** — single data points are noise. Compare today to yesterday, this week
   to last week, this month to last month.

## MAA: Metrics, Analysis, Action

Dennis Yu's optimization methodology, applied in 15-minute daily cycles:

### The 15-Minute MAA Cycle

```
DAILY MAA CYCLE (15 minutes)

MINUTE 1-5: METRICS
  Pull the numbers from the last 24 hours:
  - Spend: How much did we spend? On budget?
  - Engagement rate: Up or down from yesterday?
  - CPC/CPM: Trending which direction?
  - Conversions: How many? At what cost?
  - Frequency: Are we oversaturating any audience?
  - Any alerts? (Budget overspend, account flags, pixel issues)

MINUTE 6-10: ANALYSIS
  Explain the changes:
  - WHY did engagement drop? (Creative fatigue? Audience exhaustion?)
  - WHY did CPC increase? (Competition? Algorithm change? New audience?)
  - WHAT is different from yesterday? (Changed creative? New ad set?)
  - Compare to 7-day average — is this a trend or a blip?
  - The VALUE is in measuring CHANGES over time, not single data points.

MINUTE 11-15: ACTION
  Decide what to do:
  - KILL: Which ads/ad sets are below threshold? Stop them now.
  - SCALE: Which ads are performing above threshold? Increase budget.
  - TEST: Launch any new $1/day tests queued in the pipeline.
  - ADJUST: Change targeting, bids, or placements based on analysis.
  - FLAG: Identify issues that need escalation (funnel problems, offer issues).
```

### AI Agent Advantage in MAA

An AI agent system transforms the MAA cycle from a manual human process to a
computational one:

- **Metrics**: Ingest data instantly from performance exports or API connections.
  No manual spreadsheet pulling.
- **Analysis**: Compute statistical significance, identify patterns across dozens
  of ad sets simultaneously, detect trends that human reviewers miss.
- **Action**: Generate specific, prioritized action lists with clear rationale.
  "Kill Ad Set 7 (4.2% engagement, day 8), scale Ad Set 12 from $5 to $7
  (11.3% engagement, stable 5 days), launch 3 new WHY video tests queued
  from last week's content batch."

## Budget Allocation Framework: Test → Validate → Scale

### Stage 1: Test ($1/Day Per Variant)
- Run 10-20 ad variants simultaneously.
- Total testing budget: $10-$20/day ($70-$140/week).
- Duration: 7 days per round.
- Kill 90% of underperformers after each round.
- This is the most important stage — it is where you find winners.

### Stage 2: Validate ($5-$30/Day Per Winner)
- Take the top 1-2 performers from testing.
- Increase to $5/day for another 7 days.
- If performance holds, increase to $30/day for 30 days.
- Validate across different audience segments.
- Confirm downstream conversion metrics (not just engagement).

### Stage 3: Scale ($30-$100+/Day Per Proven Winner)
- Only scale fully validated winners.
- Increase budget by no more than 20-30% per adjustment.
- Monitor frequency and audience saturation continuously.
- Layer in lookalike audiences for fresh reach at scale.
- Continue testing new content at $1/day in parallel — never stop the pipeline.

### Budget Split by Account Maturity

| Account Stage | Test % | Validate % | Scale % | Reasoning                           |
|---------------|--------|------------|---------|-------------------------------------|
| New (0-3 mo)  | 40%    | 35%        | 25%     | Need to find what works first       |
| Growth (3-12) | 25%    | 30%        | 45%     | Have some winners, building more    |
| Mature (12+)  | 15%    | 25%        | 60%     | Many proven winners, optimize scale |

## The GCT Framework: Goals, Content, Targeting

GCT is the strategic backbone of Dennis Yu's entire marketing methodology:

### Goals
- Every campaign starts with clearly defined, quantifiable business goals.
- Goals exist at each funnel stage: awareness, engagement, conversion.
- Each goal has two metrics: **cost per goal** and **goal occurrences**.
  - Example: Cost per lead = $15, target 200 leads/month.
  - Example: Cost per purchase = $45, target 100 purchases/month.
- Goals must reflect true business outcomes — not vanity metrics.
- GCT is channel-independent: the same goals apply whether running Meta, Google, TikTok.

### Content
- Content validates mission and values through ongoing publication.
- Content types map to funnel stages (Why / How / What — see 3x3 Grid reference).
- Content should be created and tested organically before paid amplification.
- The Content Engine generates authority through consistent, valuable publishing.
- Raw, authentic content outperforms polished production in social feeds.

### Targeting
- Three forms of targeting:
  - **What** (search keywords — intent-based targeting, e.g., Google Ads)
  - **Where** (placement-based — feed, stories, reels, search results)
  - **Who** (people-based targeting — the most sustainable form)
- People-based targeting uses behavioral + demographic signals.
- **Relevancy** = the intersection of Content and Targeting.
- **Sequencing** = chaining messaging across audiences based on behavior.

## The 6-Phase Social Amplification Engine

Dennis Yu's Social Amplification Engine follows six phases:

### Phase 1: Plumbing
- Tracking infrastructure must be in place before spending money.
- Pixels, custom audiences, analytics, UTMs, conversion events — all verified.
- **AI agent action:** Run a plumbing diagnostic checklist before any campaign launch.

### Phase 2: Goals
- Define measurable outcomes at each funnel stage.
- Set cost-per-conversion targets based on unit economics.
- Establish baselines for comparison.
- **AI agent action:** Compute target CPA from LTV, margin, and allowable CAC.

### Phase 3: Content
- Produce content aligned to Why / How / What framework.
- Prioritize video (especially 1-minute vertical).
- Raw and authentic, not over-produced.
- **AI agent action:** Generate creative briefs, script outlines, and ad copy variations.

### Phase 4: Targeting
- Build audiences: custom, lookalike, interest-based, behavioral.
- Sequence people through funnel stages based on engagement behavior.
- Use remarketing to move people from awareness to consideration to conversion.
- **AI agent action:** Design audience architecture and sequencing rules.

### Phase 5: Amplification
- Apply Dollar-a-Day spend to boost proven content.
- Scale winners by increasing budget incrementally.
- Use the 3-Campaign System to structure the funnel.
- **AI agent action:** Compute budget graduation timelines, recommend scaling pace.

### Phase 6: Optimization
- Follow MAA (Metrics, Analysis, Action) daily.
- Review metrics, analyze changes, take action.
- Optimize in 15-minute daily review cycles.
- Continuously test new content while scaling winners.
- **AI agent action:** Run computational MAA cycles, generate prioritized action lists.

## The 3-Campaign System

Dennis Yu's funnel structure uses three campaign types working in concert:

### Campaign 1: Awareness (News Feed Exposure)
- **Objective:** Ensure audience sees your content.
- **Targets:** Fans, custom audiences, broad interests.
- **Content:** WHY videos, brand stories.
- **Budget:** $1/day per post being tested.
- **Metric:** Cost per ThruPlay <$0.05, video view rate >15%.

### Campaign 2: Engagement (Social Proof)
- **Objective:** Amplify to friends of engaged users, build social proof.
- **Targets:** Friends-of-fans (for pages with 10,000+ fans), engagers.
- **Content:** HOW videos, educational content.
- **Budget:** $1-5/day per content piece.
- **Metric:** Engagement rate >10%, shares, meaningful comments.

### Campaign 3: Conversion (Promotional)
- **Objective:** Drive measurable business outcomes.
- **Targets:** Warm and hot audiences from Campaigns 1 and 2.
- **Content:** WHAT videos, offers, testimonials, CTAs.
- **Budget:** Scale budget on proven content.
- **Metric:** CPA within target, ROAS above threshold.
- **Rule:** Follow the 4:1 ratio — one promotional message per four value-giving posts.

## Remarketing Sequences and Audience Building

Dollar-a-Day builds remarketing audiences at minimal cost:

### Video View Audiences
- Custom audiences based on video watch thresholds:
  - 3 seconds (minimal intent — large pool)
  - 10 seconds (some interest)
  - 15 seconds / ThruPlay (meaningful engagement — primary trigger)
  - 25%, 50%, 75%, 95% completion (increasing intent)
- WHY video viewers (15s+) → sequenced into HOW content
- HOW video viewers (15s+) → sequenced into WHAT content
- Creates an automated behavioral funnel powered by engagement

### Engagement Audiences
- People who engaged with posts, ads, or the page/profile
- Layer with website visitor audiences for multi-signal targeting
- Use for consideration and conversion campaigns

### Website Custom Audiences
- Retarget by pages visited, time on site, visit frequency
- Create audiences for specific product/service pages
- 30-day windows for warm, 7-day windows for hot/high-intent

### Lookalike Audiences
- Source: highest-value customers (top 1,000 by LTV or purchase frequency)
- Create at 1%, 2%, 5% thresholds
- Test each lookalike at $1/day before committing budget
- Refresh source audiences quarterly

## When Dollar-a-Day Doesn't Work (and What to Do Instead)

Dollar-a-Day is not universally applicable. It fails or underperforms in these scenarios:

### Scenario 1: No Existing Content or Authority
- **Problem:** Dollar-a-Day amplifies what already works. If there is no organic content,
  no customer base, no brand presence — there is nothing to amplify.
- **Fix:** Spend 30-60 days building organic content first. Create the 3x3 Grid content
  library. Build an email list. Get initial customer testimonials. Then start Dollar-a-Day.

### Scenario 2: Broken Funnel
- **Problem:** Ads perform well (high engagement, low CPC) but nothing converts. The
  landing page, checkout, or sales process is broken.
- **Fix:** Do not increase ad spend. Fix the funnel first. Run conversion rate optimization.
  Dollar-a-Day will only send more traffic into a leaking bucket.

### Scenario 3: Bad Offer
- **Problem:** The product/service has no market demand, bad pricing, or weak value
  proposition. No amount of paid amplification will fix a bad offer.
- **Fix:** Return to offer development. Test the offer with organic content and direct
  outreach before spending on paid amplification.

### Scenario 4: Tiny Audience Size
- **Problem:** The target market is <10,000 people (extremely niche B2B, hyperlocal).
  $1/day may not generate enough impressions for meaningful data.
- **Fix:** Increase minimum test budget to $3-5/day. Use highly targeted account-based
  approaches. Consider LinkedIn over Meta for ultra-niche B2B.

### Scenario 5: Time-Sensitive Campaigns
- **Problem:** Product launch in 7 days, event next week, seasonal sale this weekend.
  Dollar-a-Day's 7-day testing cycle is too slow.
- **Fix:** Use historical data from previous Dollar-a-Day tests (if available) to inform
  the time-sensitive campaign. Or accept higher risk with larger initial budgets on
  content/audiences that have proven in past tests.

## Real Campaign Examples with Numbers

### Example 1: Local Dental Practice
- **Starting point:** 3 Facebook posts per week, no paid spend.
- **Organic winners identified:** 2 posts with 8%+ engagement (patient testimonial video,
  team culture photo).
- **Dollar-a-Day test:** Both boosted at $1/day for 7 days.
  - Patient testimonial video: 14.2% engagement rate, $0.03 CPT. WINNER.
  - Team culture photo: 6.1% engagement rate. KILLED.
- **Scaled:** Video → $5/day (engagement held at 12.8%) → $30/day (engagement at 11.1%).
- **Result:** 47 new patient inquiries in 60 days from $1,050 total spend. CPA: $22.34.

### Example 2: SaaS B2B Company
- **Starting point:** LinkedIn thought leadership posts, no paid.
- **Organic winners:** CTO's post about industry trend got 3x average engagement.
- **Dollar-a-Day test:** Boosted on LinkedIn at $1/day for 7 days.
  - Engagement rate: 8.7% (strong for LinkedIn B2B). Promoted to $5/day.
- **Audience expansion:** Tested against 3 new interest-based audiences.
  - Audience A: 9.2% engagement. SCALED.
  - Audience B: 4.1%. KILLED.
  - Audience C: 7.8%. KEPT, tested with different creative.
- **Result:** 23 demo requests in 45 days from $680 total spend. CPA: $29.57.

### Example 3: E-commerce DTC Brand
- **Starting point:** Instagram content, 12k followers, some organic traction.
- **3x3 Grid test:** Created 9 videos (3 WHY, 3 HOW, 3 WHAT). Tested all at $1/day.
  - WHY video 2 (founder story): 16.3% engagement. UNICORN.
  - HOW video 1 (styling tips): 11.7% engagement. WINNER.
  - WHAT video 3 (product demo with pricing): 3.8% engagement with cold audience. KILLED
    at cold, retested with warm audience built from WHY/HOW viewers → 9.4% engagement. KEPT.
- **Full funnel built:** WHY → HOW → WHAT sequence over 21 days.
- **Result:** $4,200 total spend over 90 days, $18,900 revenue attributed. ROAS: 4.5x.

### Example 4: Professional Services (Consultant)

## Key Principles Summary

1. **Never spend big before testing small.** $1/day is your proof of concept.
2. **Amplify what already works organically.** Organic winners are pre-validated content.
3. **Kill 90% of tests ruthlessly.** Only scale the top 10%. This is not failure — this is
   the system working as designed.
4. **The 10% engagement rate is the quality signal.** Binary decision: keep or kill.
5. **Sequencing beats blasting.** Move people through funnel stages with remarketing, do
   not blast the same message to everyone.
6. **Raw, authentic content outperforms polished ads.** People engage with people, not
   productions. Use your phone camera.
7. **Plumbing must be set up before any spend.** Broken tracking = wasted budget.
8. **Goals drive everything.** GCT order matters: Goals first, then Content, then Targeting.
9. **Optimize daily with MAA.** 15 minutes: Metrics, Analysis, Action. Every day.
10. **Dollar-a-Day is a mindset, not a budget.** Test everything at minimum viable cost
    before committing real resources.
