# Dennis Yu's 3x3 Video Grid Strategy — Complete Framework

## Reference Document for Media Planner Agent

---

## Origin and Purpose

The 3x3 Video Grid is Dennis Yu's framework for creating a complete marketing funnel
using nine short videos (minimum — more variants per cell are recommended). The grid maps
three content types (Why, How, What) against three audience temperature levels (Cold, Warm,
Hot), producing nine combinations that systematically move prospects from awareness through
consideration to conversion.

The framework is built on a fundamental insight from Dennis Yu's analysis of billions in
ad spend: **people need to know you, like you, and trust you before they buy from you.**
The 3x3 Grid systematizes that journey using video content sequenced through behavioral
remarketing, instead of hoping a single ad convinces a stranger to buy immediately.

### Why This Framework Exists

Most paid media strategies fail because they:
1. Try to sell to cold audiences who have never heard of the brand
2. Run the same message to everyone regardless of their relationship stage
3. Do not sequence content — they blast and hope
4. Have no system for moving people through awareness → consideration → conversion

The 3x3 Grid solves all four problems by mapping specific content to specific audiences
at specific stages, with behavioral triggers that automatically advance people through
the funnel.

## The Three Content Types (Columns)

### Column 1: WHY Videos — Attraction and Awareness

**Purpose:** Get attention, establish authority, create a meaningful first impression.
People buy your WHY. They do not buy what you sell — they buy why you sell it.

**Content:** Personal stories from the founder, team members, customers, and anyone
authoritative to the target audience. Mission-driven narratives that answer: "Why does
this business exist? Why should I care? Why are these people worth my attention?"

**Tone:** Personal, vulnerable, mission-driven. Not promotional. Not corporate. Real.

**Maps to:**
- Know (in the Know-Like-Trust sequence)
- Awareness (in the Awareness-Consideration-Conversion funnel)
- Top of Funnel (TOF) behavior

**WHY Video Formula (1 minute each):**

```
SECOND 0-3:   HOOK
              Visual or verbal hook that stops the scroll.
              "The moment I knew everything had to change was..."

SECOND 3-43:  "WHEN I WAS..." (40 seconds)
              Tell a personal story. A challenge, a turning point,
              a defining moment that shaped why you do what you do.
              Be specific. Use sensory details. Make it real.

SECOND 43-53: "I BELIEVE THAT..." (10 seconds)
              Connect the story to a business belief or value.
              Bridge from personal narrative to professional mission.
              "I believe that every homeowner deserves..."

SECOND 53-60: "I AM..." (10 seconds)
              State who you are and what you do. Simple identity
              statement that sticks. No pitch. Just a name and role.
              "I'm [Name], and I [simple description of what you do]."
```

**Critical rules for WHY videos:**
- Do NOT mention your product or service name
- Do NOT include pricing or offers
- Do NOT include a hard call to action
- DO be genuine, vulnerable, and specific
- DO tell stories, not facts
- DO shoot on your phone — raw, unpolished, real

### Column 2: HOW Videos — Engagement and Education

**Purpose:** Share expertise, demonstrate competence, build trust through generous teaching.
Show the audience you know your craft so well that you can teach it freely.

**Content:** Teach what you know. Dennis Yu's specific instruction: teach what you would
do if your product did not exist to solve the problem. Give away your best knowledge in
simple, actionable steps. Three-step recipes for solving real problems.

**Tone:** Helpful, instructional, generous with knowledge. Expert but approachable.

**Maps to:**
- Like (in the Know-Like-Trust sequence)
- Consideration (in the Awareness-Consideration-Conversion funnel)
- Middle of Funnel (MOF) behavior

**HOW Video Formula (1 minute each):**

```
SECOND 0-3:   HOOK
              State the problem or question directly.
              "Here's the #1 mistake people make with..."
              "Want to know how to [desired outcome]? 3 steps."

SECOND 3-10:  PROBLEM STATEMENT (7 seconds)
              Clearly define the problem the audience faces.
              Make them nod. "This happens to you, right?"

SECOND 10-50: THREE STEPS TO SOLVE IT (40 seconds)
              Step 1: [Specific, actionable instruction]
              Step 2: [Specific, actionable instruction]
              Step 3: [Specific, actionable instruction]
              Show, don't just tell. Demonstrate when possible.

SECOND 50-60: SUMMARIZE KEY TAKEAWAY (10 seconds)
              "So remember: [Step 1], [Step 2], [Step 3].
              That's how you [outcome]."
```

**Critical rules for HOW videos:**
- Do NOT talk about your product or service. Teach the skill, not your solution.
- Do NOT mention pricing or push for a sale
- DO give genuinely useful, actionable advice
- DO teach something the viewer can implement immediately
- DO demonstrate competence — this is where expertise builds trust
- DO use simple language, not jargon

### Column 3: WHAT Videos — Conversion and Selling

**Purpose:** Present offers, features, pricing, testimonials, and calls to action. This
is where you sell — but only to audiences who have already been warmed through WHY and HOW
content.

**Content:** Product demonstrations, customer testimonials, pricing breakdowns, case studies
with specific results, special offers, free trial invitations, booking CTAs.

**Tone:** Direct, confident, proof-driven. Not aggressive or desperate. The audience
already knows and likes you — now show them you are worth buying from.

**Maps to:**
- Trust (in the Know-Like-Trust sequence)
- Conversion (in the Awareness-Consideration-Conversion funnel)
- Bottom of Funnel (BOF) behavior

**WHAT Video Formula (1 minute each):**

```
SECOND 0-3:   HOOK
              State the result or outcome directly.
              "Our clients typically see [specific result] within
              [specific timeframe]."

SECOND 3-10:  OFFER STATEMENT (7 seconds)
              "Here's what we built to solve [the problem you
              taught about in HOW videos]."

SECOND 10-50: PROOF (40 seconds)
              Option A: Customer testimonial (their words, their face)
              Option B: Product demo (show it working, show results)
              Option C: Case study (specific numbers, specific client)
              Use the most compelling proof format available.

SECOND 50-60: CLEAR CALL TO ACTION (10 seconds)
              "Click the link below to [specific action]."
              "Book a free consultation at [URL]."
              "Use code [X] for [discount] — this week only."
              Be specific. One action. No ambiguity.
```

**Critical rules for WHAT videos:**
- Only show to audiences who have engaged with WHY and/or HOW content
- DO include specific proof (numbers, testimonials, demonstrations)
- DO include a clear, single call to action
- DO create urgency when authentic (limited availability, seasonal pricing)
- Do NOT lead with the price — lead with the proof, then present the price
- Do NOT show to cold audiences (they have not earned trust yet)

## The Three Audience Temperatures (Rows)

### Row 1: Cold Audience — Top of Funnel (TOF)

**Definition:** People who have never interacted with your brand. They do not know you
exist. They have zero relationship with you.

**Targeting methods:**
- Interest-based audiences (related topics, competitors, industry publications)
- Lookalike audiences (1-5% based on best customers)
- Broad demographic targeting (age, location, gender if relevant)
- In 2025-2026: Advantage+ broad targeting with creative as the signal

**Goal:** Earn the right to continue the conversation. Introduce yourself. Create a
positive first impression that is memorable enough to recognize you next time.

**Metric focus:**
- Cost per ThruPlay (target: <$0.05)
- Video view rate (target: >15%)
- Reach and frequency (reach high, frequency low — 1-2x/week max)
- 3-second view rate (is the hook stopping the scroll?)

**What cold audiences see:** WHY videos first. Possibly broad HOW videos. Never WHAT
videos (selling to strangers does not work and damages brand perception).

### Row 2: Warm Audience — Middle of Funnel (MOF)

**Definition:** People who have engaged with your content but have not converted. They
know you exist. They have shown some interest. They are considering.

**Targeting methods:**
- Custom audiences: video viewers (15s+ on any WHY video)
- Custom audiences: page/profile engagers (30-day window)
- Custom audiences: website visitors (30-day window)
- Email list members who have not purchased
- In 2025-2026: Engagement custom audiences with layered signals

**Goal:** Deepen the relationship. Demonstrate expertise. Move from "I've heard of them"
to "I trust their knowledge and judgment."

**Metric focus:**
- Engagement rate (target: 8-10%+)
- Click-through rate (target: >2%)
- Cost per engagement
- Video completion rate
- Comment quality (substantive vs. superficial)

**What warm audiences see:** HOW videos (primary) + continued WHY videos (reinforcement).
Soft WHAT content (use cases, not hard sells) is also appropriate.

### Row 3: Hot Audience — Bottom of Funnel (BOF)

**Definition:** People who have shown strong intent. They have watched multiple videos,
visited key pages, added to cart, started but not completed a form, downloaded a lead
magnet, attended a webinar. They are ready — they just need the final push.

**Targeting methods:**
- Custom audiences: video viewers (15s+ on any HOW video)
- Custom audiences: website visitors to product/pricing pages (7-day window)
- Custom audiences: cart abandoners (7-day window)
- Custom audiences: form starters who did not complete
- Multi-touchpoint engagers (engaged with 3+ pieces of content)

**Goal:** Close. Drive the conversion. Get the lead, the sale, the booking, the sign-up.

**Metric focus:**
- Cost per conversion (primary — the only metric that matters at BOF)
- Conversion rate
- ROAS (return on ad spend)
- View-through conversions
- Click-through conversions

**What hot audiences see:** WHAT videos (primary) + continued HOW videos (reinforcement,
objection handling). Testimonials (Cell 7) are critical here.

                  Brand awareness      Educational intros    Usually SKIPPED
                  Origin stories       Broad tips            for cold — too
                  Mission videos       Industry basics       early to sell

                  ──────────────────    ──────────────────    ──────────────────

                  Deeper founder       Detailed tutorials    Soft product
                  stories, team        Step-by-step guides   introductions
                  culture, values      Expert knowledge      Use cases, demos
                  PRIORITY: MEDIUM     PRIORITY: HIGH        PRIORITY: MEDIUM
                  ──────────────────    ──────────────────    ──────────────────

                  Customer success     Implementation        Direct offers
                  stories, social      guides, "how we       Pricing, demos

                  PRIORITY: HIGH       PRIORITY: MEDIUM      PRIORITY: HIGH

### Cell 1: Cold + Why (PRIORITY: HIGH — Start Here)

**Purpose:** Introduce the brand to strangers through authentic storytelling. This is the
entry point of the entire funnel. Without Cell 1, there is no funnel.

**Content specifics:**
- Founder origin story: "When I was [pivotal moment], I realized [insight]..."
- Company mission: "We exist because [problem] is unacceptable."
- Industry belief: "I believe [contrarian or passionate stance about the industry]."
- 3 separate videos, each telling a different story from a different angle.

**Format:** 1-minute vertical video (9:16). Shot on phone. No intro bumper, no logo
animation, no corporate music. Just a person speaking to camera.

**Targeting:** Broad interest-based audiences OR 1% lookalike based on best customers.
In 2025-2026, broad targeting with Advantage+ often outperforms interest targeting here.

**Campaign objective:** Video Views / ThruPlay.

**Budget:** $1/day per video. 3 videos = $3/day = $21/week total.

**Success metrics:**
- Cost per ThruPlay: <$0.05
- Video view rate: >15%
- 3-second view rate: >40% (hook effectiveness)
- Engagement rate: >5% (lower bar for cold — they do not know you yet)

**Failure signals:**
- Cost per ThruPlay >$0.10 — hook is not working, content is not resonating
- View rate <10% — content feels like an ad, not like content
- 3-second rate <25% — the opening hook is failing to stop the scroll

**Example:** A roofing contractor tells the story of the first house he fixed after a
tornado destroyed his neighbor's home for free. Raw, emotional, specific. No mention of
his roofing company name, pricing, or services. Just the story.

### Cell 2: Cold + How (PRIORITY: MEDIUM)

**Purpose:** Demonstrate expertise to cold audiences through broadly applicable education.
Surface-level tips that show competence without requiring brand familiarity.

**Content specifics:**
- "3 things every [target audience] should know about [topic]"
- "The #1 mistake [target audience] makes with [topic] (and how to avoid it)"
- Industry myth-busting: "You've been told [common belief]. Here's the truth."
- Tips that are genuinely useful even if the viewer never becomes a customer.

**Format:** 1-minute vertical video. Can include simple on-screen text, demonstrations,
or props. Still shot on phone — keep it authentic.

**Targeting:** Same broad targeting as Cell 1.

**Campaign objective:** Video Views / Engagement.

**Budget:** $1/day per video.

**Success metrics:**
- Engagement rate: >5%
- Shares and saves (high-intent actions for educational content)
- Comment quality: "I didn't know that!" or "Great tip!"

**Example:** A financial advisor explains: "3 tax deductions most small business owners
miss." No mention of his firm, his services, or his pricing. Just pure value.

### Cell 3: Cold + What (PRIORITY: LOW — Usually Skip)

**Purpose:** Direct response to cold audiences. Rarely effective. Most practitioners
skip this cell entirely.

**When to use Cell 3:**
- High-demand/seasonal products where intent already exists (Black Friday, holiday gifts)
- Products with mass appeal and low price point (<$20)
- Brand-level awareness ads (not direct response) for large companies with recognition
- Retargeting website visitors who arrived via search (they have intent, even if cold on social)

**When to skip Cell 3:** Almost always. Selling to strangers on social media without any
relationship building has the highest CPA and lowest ROAS of any cell in the grid.

**Example of when it works:** A well-known brand running a 50% off holiday sale can show
Cell 3 ads to cold audiences because the brand recognition already exists from other
channels (TV, search, word of mouth).

### Cell 4: Warm + Why (PRIORITY: MEDIUM)

**Purpose:** Deepen the brand story for people who have already shown interest. Move from
"I've seen this brand" to "I feel connected to these people."

**Content specifics:**
- Deeper founder stories: specific challenges, failures, lessons learned
- Team member introductions: "Meet [person], who [compelling personal detail]"
- Behind-the-scenes: company culture, how the product is made, daily operations
- Values in action: how the company lives its stated mission in real situations

**Targeting:** Custom audience — people who watched any Cell 1 or Cell 2 video for 15+
seconds. This is a behavioral trigger: they showed interest, so they earn deeper content.

**Campaign objective:** Engagement.

**Budget:** $1/day per video.

**Success metrics:**
- Engagement rate: >8%
- Comment quality: personal responses, questions, emotional reactions
- Profile visits and follows (people want to know more)

**Example:** The roofing contractor introduces his lead installer, who tells the story of
rebuilding homes after Hurricane Harvey. More depth, more team, more human connection.

### Cell 5: Warm + How (PRIORITY: HIGH)

**Purpose:** This is the trust-building engine. Detailed expertise that proves the brand
genuinely knows what it is doing. After Cell 1 made them aware and Cell 4 made them
interested, Cell 5 makes them think "these people really know their stuff."

**Content specifics:**
- Detailed, multi-step tutorials that solve real problems
- "Here's exactly how to [desired outcome], step by step"
- Demonstrate the process, not just describe it
- Go deeper than Cell 2 — this audience is invested and wants substance

**Targeting:** Custom audience — Cell 1/2 video viewers (15s+) + page engagers (30 days).

**Campaign objective:** Engagement / Traffic (drive to blog posts, YouTube videos, or
lead magnets).

**Budget:** $1/day test → scale to $5-30/day for winners.

**Success metrics:**
- Engagement rate: >10% (the Dennis Yu benchmark)
- Click-through rate: >2% (if driving to content)
- Time on landing page (if driving to blog/resource)
- Video completion rate: >40%

**Example:** The financial advisor walks through: "How to Calculate Your Tax-Adjusted
Investment Returns: 3-Step Process." Specific, actionable, expert-level content that
positions him as the authority.

### Cell 6: Warm + What (PRIORITY: MEDIUM)

**Purpose:** Soft product introduction. The bridge between education and selling. "Now
that you've seen our expertise, here's what we built to solve this problem."

**Content specifics:**
- Product/service overview tied to the problems taught in Cell 5
- Use case walkthroughs: "Here's how [customer type] uses [product] to [outcome]"
- Feature demonstrations that connect to previously taught knowledge
- NOT hard selling — positioning, not pushing

**Targeting:** Custom audience — HOW video viewers (15s+ on Cell 5 content).

**Campaign objective:** Traffic / Lead Generation.

**Budget:** $1/day test.

**Success metrics:**
- Click-through rate: >2%
- Landing page conversion rate (if driving to opt-in/demo request)
- Lead quality (are leads from this cell qualified?)

**Example:** The financial advisor says: "I showed you how to calculate tax-adjusted
returns. Here's the tool we built that does it automatically in 30 seconds. Let me show
you how it works." Natural bridge from teaching to product.

### Cell 7: Hot + Why (PRIORITY: HIGH)

**Purpose:** Third-party proof. Customer testimonials and success stories that reinforce
the brand's WHY through someone else's voice. At the hot stage, prospects trust other
customers more than they trust the brand itself.

**Content specifics:**
- Customer testimonials: specific results, specific timelines, real faces
- Case studies told as stories: "We worked with [client], who had [problem]..."
- Social proof: awards, media mentions, certifications, partnerships
- Before/after demonstrations with real customers

**Targeting:** Custom audience — Cell 5/6 video viewers (15s+), website visitors (product
pages, pricing page), cart abandoners.

**Campaign objective:** Conversions (with testimonial as the creative).

**Budget:** $1/day test → scale for winners.

**Success metrics:**
- Assisted conversions (this cell often drives conversions attributed to Cell 9)
- View-through conversions
- Comment sentiment: "I had the same experience!" or "Convinced me."

**Example:** A customer of the roofing contractor says on camera: "They replaced our roof
in 2 days, were $3,000 less than the other quote, and cleaned up the yard better than it
looked before. I've sent them 4 referrals since." Real person, specific numbers, genuine
endorsement.

### Cell 8: Hot + How (PRIORITY: MEDIUM)

**Purpose:** Reduce friction and fear of buying. Show the buying/onboarding process so
prospects know exactly what to expect. Address objections through transparency.

**Content specifics:**
- "How it works when you become a client" — walk through the process step by step
- "What to expect in your first 30 days" — set expectations
- FAQ-style content addressing common objections and concerns
- Implementation/onboarding previews

**Targeting:** Same hot audience as Cell 7.

**Campaign objective:** Conversions.

**Budget:** $1/day test.

**Success metrics:**
- Conversion rate lift when included in the sequence (A/B test with and without Cell 8)
- Reduced objection mentions in sales conversations
- Lower drop-off between inquiry and purchase/sign-up

**Example:** The financial advisor walks through: "Here's exactly what happens when you
book a consultation. First call: 15 minutes, no cost, we review your situation. If it's
a fit, here's what the plan looks like. If not, I'll point you in the right direction.
No pressure." Removes fear, shows transparency.

### Cell 9: Hot + What (PRIORITY: HIGH)

**Purpose:** Close the deal. Direct offer to the hottest audience — people who have been
through WHY and HOW content, watched testimonials, visited the website. They are ready.

**Content specifics:**
- Direct offer: pricing, packages, specific next step
- Product/service demo with clear benefits and outcomes
- Limited-time offers (if authentic — not manufactured urgency)
- Free trial, free consultation, risk-free guarantee
- Strong, clear, single call to action

**Targeting:** Hottest audience — multi-stage engagers who have watched WHY + HOW +
testimonial content, visited product/pricing pages, or shown high-intent signals.

**Campaign objective:** Conversions (optimized for purchase, lead, or booking).

**Budget:** $1/day test → scale aggressively for proven performers (this cell has the
highest potential ROAS because the audience is most qualified).

**Success metrics:**
- Cost per conversion (THE metric for this cell)
- ROAS (return on ad spend)
- Conversion rate
- Cost per acquisition (CPA)

**Example:** The roofing contractor: "We're booking spring roof inspections. Free
inspection, honest assessment, written quote within 24 hours. If your roof was built
before 2015, you may be eligible for insurance coverage. Click below to book your free
inspection this week." Direct, clear, risk-free offer to an audience that already
trusts him from Cells 1-8.

## Why Video Specifically

Dennis Yu's case for video-first paid strategy:

### The Data Case
- Video outperforms static images on engagement rate by 2-3x across platforms
- Video ThruPlay (15s+) creates the strongest custom audiences for remarketing
- Facebook/Meta's algorithm favors video content in distribution (more organic reach)
- Video view audiences are the most cost-effective remarketing pool to build
  ($0.01-0.05 per ThruPlay vs. $0.50-2.00+ per website visitor)
- Video completion rates provide granular behavioral data for audience segmentation

### The Trust Case
- Video conveys tone, personality, and authenticity in ways text and images cannot
- Seeing a real person speak builds trust faster than reading polished copy
- Video stories are remembered 22x better than facts alone
- The WHY video formula creates emotional connection that text cannot replicate
- Video testimonials (Cell 7) are the most powerful trust signal in digital marketing

### The Platform Case (2025-2026)
- Every major platform (Meta, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn) is prioritizing video
- Reels, Shorts, and vertical video formats dominate feed placement
- Creative-as-targeting: video teaches the algorithm who your audience is more
  effectively than static images because of the richer behavioral signals
  (watch time, completion rate, replays)
- Advantage+ campaigns perform best with video-diverse creative libraries

### Production Approach
Dennis Yu is explicit: **overproduction kills performance.** The guidelines:

| Do                               | Don't                                 |
|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Shoot on your phone              | Hire a video production crew          |
| Natural lighting, real locations | Studio with professional lighting     |
| Speak naturally, conversational  | Read from a teleprompter              |
| One take (or minimal retakes)    | Multiple takes edited to "perfection" |
| Add captions (for sound-off)     | Add music tracks or sound effects     |
| 60 seconds or less               | 3-5 minute "mini-documentaries"       |
| Eye contact with camera lens     | Looking at yourself on screen         |

**Why raw works:** Polished video looks like an advertisement. People have been trained
to ignore advertisements. Raw, phone-shot video looks like content from a friend — it
triggers social engagement, not ad avoidance.

## How to Sequence the Grid (Implementation Order)

Dennis Yu recommends building the grid in this specific order, not all at once:

### Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1-2)

```
PHASE 1: COLD + WHY (Cell 1)

Day 1-2:  Record 3 WHY videos (3 different stories/angles)
Day 3:    Upload and launch all 3 as $1/day boosts
          Target: Broad audience or 1% lookalike
Day 3-10: Monitor daily (MAA cycle)
Day 10:   Evaluate:
          - Which WHY video hit 10%+ engagement?
          - Which had best cost per ThruPlay?
          - Which generated positive comments?
          KILL losers. KEEP winner(s).
Day 10:   Build Custom Audience: "People who watched
          winning WHY video for 15+ seconds" (30-day window)

OUTCOME: 1 proven WHY video + warm audience pool
COST: $21 (3 videos x $7 each)
```

### Phase 2: Education (Week 3-4)

```
PHASE 2: WARM + HOW (Cell 5)

Day 15-16: Record 3 HOW videos (3 different topics/skills)
Day 17:    Upload and launch all 3 as $1/day boosts
           Target: Custom audience from Phase 1 (WHY viewers 15s+)
Day 17-24: Monitor daily (MAA cycle)
Day 24:    Evaluate:
           - Which HOW video hit 10%+ engagement?
           - Which drove clicks or saves?
           - Which generated "thank you" or "great tip" comments?
           KILL losers. KEEP winner(s).
Day 24:    Build Custom Audience: "People who watched
           winning HOW video for 15+ seconds" (30-day window)

OUTCOME: 1 proven HOW video + hot audience pool
COST: $21 (3 videos x $7 each)
RUNNING TOTAL: $42
```

### Phase 3: Conversion (Week 5-6)

```
PHASE 3: HOT + WHAT (Cell 9)

Day 29-30: Record 3 WHAT videos (3 different offers/proof angles)
Day 31:    Upload and launch all 3 as $1/day boosts
           Target: Custom audience from Phase 2 (HOW viewers 15s+)
Day 31-38: Monitor daily (MAA cycle)
Day 38:    Evaluate:
           - Which WHAT video drove conversions?
           - Cost per conversion within target?
           - Positive purchase/inquiry sentiment?
           KILL losers. KEEP winner(s).

OUTCOME: Complete WHY → HOW → WHAT funnel operational
COST: $21 (3 videos x $7 each)
RUNNING TOTAL: $63 for a complete 3-stage funnel
```

### Phase 4: Fill the Grid (Week 7+)

Add remaining cells in this priority order:
1. **Cell 7** (Hot + Why — testimonials) — highest-impact addition to boost conversions
2. **Cell 4** (Warm + Why — deeper stories) — strengthens relationship building
3. **Cell 2** (Cold + How — educational intros) — broadens top of funnel
4. **Cell 6** (Warm + What — soft product intros) — bridges education to selling
5. **Cell 8** (Hot + How — implementation guides) — reduces purchase friction
6. **Cell 3** (Cold + What) — only if high-demand/seasonal product warrants it

## Retargeting Architecture Mapped to the Grid

The automation that powers the 3x3 Grid:

### Audience Movement Rules

```
AUDIENCE FLOW DIAGRAM

COLD AUDIENCE (Strangers)
  │
  │  Sees: Cell 1 (WHY) + Cell 2 (HOW intro)
  │
  ▼  TRIGGER: Watches any WHY/HOW video for 15+ seconds
  │
WARM AUDIENCE (Interested)
  │
  │  Sees: Cell 4 (deeper WHY) + Cell 5 (detailed HOW) + Cell 6 (soft WHAT)
  │  Also continues seeing: WHY content as reinforcement
  │
  ▼  TRIGGER: Watches any HOW video for 15+ seconds
  │         OR visits website
  │         OR engages with 3+ pieces of content
  │
HOT AUDIENCE (Ready to buy)
  │
  │  Sees: Cell 7 (testimonials) + Cell 8 (how we work) + Cell 9 (offer/CTA)
  │  Also continues seeing: HOW content as reinforcement
  │
  ▼  TRIGGER: Clicks CTA, visits product/pricing page, fills form
  │
CONVERTED (Customer)
  │
  │  EXCLUDE from acquisition funnel
  │  Move to: Loyalty sequence, upsell campaigns, referral asks
  │
  ▼
ADVOCATE (Long-term)
```

### Implementation Details

**Custom Audiences to Create:**

| Audience Name              | Source                        | Window | Used For        |
|----------------------------|-------------------------------|--------|-----------------|
| WHY Viewers 15s+           | Video view: WHY videos, 15s+  | 30 day | Warm targeting   |
| HOW Viewers 15s+           | Video view: HOW videos, 15s+  | 30 day | Hot targeting    |
| Page Engagers              | Page engagement: all actions   | 30 day | Warm targeting   |
| Website Visitors - All     | Pixel: all pages              | 30 day | Warm targeting   |
| Website Visitors - Product | Pixel: product/pricing pages  | 7 day  | Hot targeting    |
| Cart Abandoners            | Pixel: cart, no purchase      | 7 day  | Hot targeting    |
| Converters                 | Pixel: purchase/lead event    | 180 day| Exclusion + LAL |
| Multi-Touch Engagers       | Combined: 3+ touchpoints      | 30 day | Hot targeting    |
| Email List - Non-Buyers    | Customer file upload          | N/A    | Warm targeting   |
| Lookalike 1% - Customers   | Based on Converters audience  | N/A    | Cold targeting   |
| Lookalike 2% - Customers   | Based on Converters audience  | N/A    | Cold targeting   |

**Exclusion Rules:**
- Exclude Converters from all acquisition campaigns (Cells 1-9)
- Exclude Warm audiences from Cold campaigns (avoid double-serving)
- Exclude Hot audiences from Warm campaigns (avoid backward sequencing)
- If someone converts from Cell 7 (testimonial), exclude from Cell 9 (they already bought)

**Overlap Policy:** Intentional overlap between adjacent stages:
- Warm audiences see BOTH WHY (reinforcement) and HOW (progression) content
- Hot audiences see BOTH HOW (reinforcement) and WHAT (conversion) content
- This creates a surround-sound effect that builds conviction

### Audience Window Recommendations

| Audience Type        | Window Length | Reasoning                                     |
|----------------------|--------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Video view audiences | 30 days      | Standard retention window; long enough for     |
|                      |              | multi-touch exposure, short enough for intent  |
| Website visitors     | 30 days warm | General interest signal                        |
|                      | 7 days hot   | High-intent signal for recent visitors         |
| Engagement audiences | 30 days      | Active interest in content                     |
| Cart abandoners      | 7 days       | Short window — intent decays rapidly           |
| Customer exclusion   | 180 days     | Long window to avoid re-acquiring recent buyers|

**Adjust windows for sales cycle length:**
- Short cycle (impulse buy, e-commerce): 7-14 day hot windows
- Medium cycle (services, SaaS): 14-30 day hot windows
- Long cycle (B2B enterprise, real estate): 30-90 day hot windows

## Budget Distribution Across the 9 Cells

### Testing Phase (All Cells at $1/Day)

Total grid test budget: 9 cells x $1/day = $9/day = $63/week.
With 3 variants per cell: 27 total ads x $1/day = $27/day = $189/week.
This is the maximum testing investment for a complete grid.

### Scaled Phase (Winners Only)

Typical budget distribution after testing identifies winners:

```
BUDGET DISTRIBUTION BY FUNNEL STAGE

Total monthly budget: $3,000 (example)

COLD (50% = $1,500/month = $50/day)
  Cell 1 (WHY):  $35/day  (70% of cold budget — primary feeder)
  Cell 2 (HOW):  $15/day  (30% of cold budget — supplementary)
  Cell 3 (WHAT): $0/day   (skipped for most businesses)

WARM (30% = $900/month = $30/day)
  Cell 4 (WHY):  $5/day   (17% of warm budget — reinforcement)
  Cell 5 (HOW):  $18/day  (60% of warm budget — trust engine)
  Cell 6 (WHAT): $7/day   (23% of warm budget — soft bridge)

HOT (20% = $600/month = $20/day)
  Cell 7 (WHY):  $8/day   (40% of hot budget — social proof is critical)
  Cell 8 (HOW):  $2/day   (10% of hot budget — friction reducer)
  Cell 9 (WHAT): $10/day  (50% of hot budget — direct conversion)
```

**Key principle:** More budget goes to the top of funnel because cold audiences are the
largest pool and need the most reach investment. Without Cell 1 feeding the funnel,
Cells 5-9 starve. But Cell 9 gets the highest per-audience-member investment because
each impression has the highest conversion probability.

## Common Mistakes in Grid Implementation

### Mistake 1: Selling to Cold Audiences
**What happens:** Marketers skip Cells 1-2 and go straight to Cell 9 for cold traffic.
**Why it fails:** Cold audiences have zero trust. Conversion rates are <0.5%. CPA is 5-10x
higher than when following the full sequence. Ad fatigue is rapid because the audience
does not find the offer relevant.
**Fix:** Start with Cell 1. Build the relationship. Earn the right to sell.

### Mistake 2: Over-Producing Videos
**What happens:** Company hires a video production crew. Professional lighting, scripts,
teleprompters, b-roll, licensed music, lower-third graphics.
**Why it fails:** The resulting video looks like a TV commercial. Users have been trained
for 20+ years to skip commercials. Professional-looking ads trigger ad avoidance behavior.
Cost per ThruPlay is 3-5x higher than raw content.
**Fix:** Shoot on your phone. One take. Look at the camera. Be a person, not a brand.

### Mistake 3: Not Sequencing
**What happens:** All 9 videos run to all audiences simultaneously.
**Why it fails:** A cold audience sees a testimonial (Cell 7) before they know who you are.
A hot audience sees an origin story (Cell 1) when they are ready to buy. The entire logic
of the grid is destroyed. Performance is uniformly mediocre.
**Fix:** Implement the retargeting architecture. Each audience temperature sees only the
content appropriate to their stage. This is the entire point of the grid.

### Mistake 4: Scaling Too Fast
**What happens:** Cell 1 video gets 12% engagement at $1/day. Marketer jumps to $50/day.
**Why it fails:** Algorithm resets learning. Audience pool may not support the jump.
Frequency spikes. Engagement rate crashes. CPA doubles.
**Fix:** Follow the graduation staircase: $1 → $5 → $30 → incremental 20% increases.

### Mistake 5: Judging Wrong Metrics per Cell
**What happens:** Marketer judges Cell 1 (cold + why) by cost per conversion, or Cell 9
(hot + what) by video view rate.
**Why it fails:** Each cell has different objectives and therefore different success metrics.
Cell 1 succeeds on awareness metrics (CPT, view rate). Cell 9 succeeds on conversion
metrics (CPA, ROAS). Measuring the wrong metric leads to killing good ads and keeping bad ones.
**Fix:** Use the per-cell success metrics defined in this document. Each cell is measured
by the metrics appropriate to its funnel stage.

### Mistake 6: Not Refreshing Content
**What happens:** The same 9 videos run for 6 months without new creative.
**Why it fails:** Creative fatigue. Frequency increases. Engagement rate declines over time
even for previously winning content. The audience has seen the same story too many times.
**Fix:** Plan to create new grid content every 60-90 days. This does not mean replacing
everything — rotate out the weakest performers and introduce fresh stories, tips, and
offers. Keep the grid structure permanent; refresh the content within it.

### Mistake 7: Only One Video Per Cell
**What happens:** Marketer creates exactly 9 videos — one per cell — and considers the
grid complete.
**Why it fails:** With only one video per cell, there are no variants to test. The first
video might not be the best performer. There is no fallback when creative fatigues. The
grid lacks depth.
**Fix:** Create minimum 3 videos per cell (9 cells x 3 = 27 total). Test all 3 at $1/day.
Keep the best performer, kill the rest. Cycle in new variants regularly.

## Integration with Dollar-a-Day Testing

The 3x3 Grid and Dollar-a-Day strategy are designed to work together:

1. **Test each cell at $1/day** before committing budget. No exceptions.
2. **Use the 10% engagement rule** to identify winners in each cell.
3. **Kill 90% of test videos** — only keep the best performer per cell.
4. **Scale winning cells individually** — not all cells need equal budget.
5. **Budget flows down the funnel:** more on cold (largest pool), less on hot (smallest
   pool, highest conversion value).
6. **Typical budget ratio:** 50% cold / 30% warm / 20% hot (adjust based on funnel
   health and audience sizes).
7. **Never stop the pipeline:** even after finding winners for all 9 cells, continue
   testing new content variants at $1/day. Winners fatigue. The next winner must be
   ready in the pipeline.

## AI Agent System Advantages for Grid Management

An AI agent system manages the 3x3 Grid more effectively than manual execution:

- **Creative generation:** Generate ad copy, script outlines, and creative briefs for all
  9 cells based on the client's business, industry, and target audience. Produce 3+
  variants per cell simultaneously.
- **Performance analysis:** Ingest performance data across all 9 cells and compute
  cross-grid health metrics (flow rates, build rates, saturation levels) that would
  take a human analyst hours to calculate.
- **Fatigue detection:** Monitor engagement rate trends across all cells and flag when
  any cell's content is approaching fatigue (engagement declining 15%+ from peak).
- **Budget optimization:** Compute optimal budget allocation across cells based on
  current performance, audience sizes, and diminishing returns thresholds.
- **Competitive intelligence:** Research competitor ad strategies through web analysis to
  identify gaps and opportunities in the client's grid content.
- **Sequencing logic:** Design and document the complete retargeting architecture with
  audience triggers, windows, and exclusion rules.
