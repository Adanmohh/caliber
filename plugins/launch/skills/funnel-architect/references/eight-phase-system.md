# Suby's 8-Phase Selling System and the Godfather Offer

## Source: "Sell Like Crazy" by Sabri Suby

Sabri Suby is the founder of King Kong, Australia's fastest-growing digital marketing
agency. His 8-phase selling system, outlined in "Sell Like Crazy," has been used across
1,067+ niches and generated over $7.8 billion in tracked revenue for clients. The system
is designed to attract, convert, and retain customers at scale using digital marketing.
This reference integrates the complete 8-Phase System with the Godfather Offer framework
and maps the integration points to Brunson's Value Ladder.

---

## The Foundational Premise: The 3% Rule

Before understanding the 8 phases, grasp this critical insight about market awareness:

```
+------------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                            |
|  3%   READY TO BUY NOW         <-- Everyone competes here |
|  17%  Gathering information, comparing options             |
|  20%  Problem-aware but not yet solution-seeking           |
|  60%  Completely unaware they even have a problem          |
|                                                            |
|  TOTAL ADDRESSABLE: 97% are NOT ready to buy              |
|  Suby's system targets the full 100%                      |
+------------------------------------------------------------+
```

Most businesses only compete for the 3%. Suby's system targets the full 97% by using
education-based marketing, lead magnets (HVCOs), and nurture sequences to move prospects
through awareness stages.

### The 4% Rule of Productivity

64% of your results come from just 4% of your activities. That 4% is marketing and
sales. The 8-Phase System focuses energy on these high-leverage activities.

## Phase 1: Understand and Identify Your Dream Buyer

### The Halo Strategy

Suby's method for deeply understanding your Dream Buyer by uncovering intimate details
behind their thinking:

| Dimension | Questions to Answer |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Greatest Fears | What keeps them up at 3am? What are they terrified of? |
| Deepest Desires | What do they secretly wish for above all else? |
| Aspirations | Where do they want to be in 1, 5, 10 years? |
| Roadblocks | What has stopped them from getting what they want? |
| Frustrations | What are they sick and tired of? What have they tried that failed? |
| Beliefs | What do they believe about your industry/solution? What's their worldview? |

### Dream Buyer Profile Exercise

1. **Demographics:** Age, gender, income, location, job title
2. **Psychographics:** Values, interests, lifestyle, personality traits
3. **Pain points:** Specific problems causing distress (with intensity scores 1-10)
4. **Desires:** Outcomes they crave (with priority scores 1-10)
5. **Objections:** Why they might NOT buy (with response strategies)
6. **Media consumption:** Where they spend time online and offline
7. **Language:** The exact words and phrases they use to describe their problems

### The Starving Crowd Concept

Find a group of people with a burning problem or desire they are willing to pay to solve.
The best market to sell to is a starving crowd -- people in urgent need. If you are
selling to people who don't desperately want what you offer, no amount of marketing
sophistication will save you.

### Common Phase 1 Mistakes

- Trying to sell to everyone instead of a specific Dream Buyer
- Making assumptions instead of doing real research (aim for 10-20 deep interviews)
- Focusing on demographics only, ignoring psychographics
- Not using the buyer's own language in marketing copy
- Skipping this phase and jumping straight to ads

## Phase 2: Create the Perfect Bait (High-Value Content Offer)

The HVCO is the "bait" that attracts your Dream Buyer. It is NOT an ad. It is free,
value-packed content that targets the 97% who are not ready to buy yet.

### HVCO Creation Framework

1. Identify the #1 problem your Dream Buyer faces
2. Create content that solves a specific piece of that problem
3. Make the title irresistible (use numbers, specificity, outcomes)
4. Deliver massive perceived value in a consumable format
5. Position your paid offer as the logical next step
6. Design a professional cover/mockup to increase perceived value

### HVCO Formats

| Format | Best For | Consumption Time |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| PDF guide / ebook | Complex topics, B2B | 15-30 minutes |
| Checklist / cheat sheet | Actionable steps, quick wins | 2-5 minutes |
| Video training | Visual learners, demonstrations | 10-30 minutes |
| Webinar | High-ticket lead gen | 45-90 minutes |
| Quiz / assessment | Segmentation, personalization | 3-5 minutes |
| Templates / swipe files | Implementation-focused | Immediate use |
| Free consultation | Service businesses, B2B | 15-30 minutes |

### Title Formulas

- "The [Number] [Mistakes/Secrets/Steps] to [Desired Outcome] Without [Pain Point]"
- "How to [Desired Outcome] in [Timeframe] Even If [Common Objection]"
- "The [Adjective] Guide to [Topic] for [Specific Audience]"

### Common Phase 2 Mistakes

- HVCO too broad or generic (must solve ONE specific problem)
- Looks cheap or low-effort (invest in professional design)
- Asking for too much information on the opt-in form
- Not connecting HVCO topic to the paid offering
- Giving away so much there is no need to buy

### Benchmarks

- Opt-in conversion rate: aim for 20-45% on landing pages
- HVCO consumption rate: track downloads vs. actual reads/views
- Lead-to-customer conversion from HVCO leads: 2-10% depending on nurture

## Phase 3: Capture Leads with Landing Pages

The landing page has ONE job: convert visitors into leads. It is not a homepage or
brochure.

### Suby's Landing Page Structure

1. **Call out your audience** -- Make it immediately obvious who this is for
2. **Big promise headline** -- Result they will get, how fast, without usual hassle
3. **Clarifying sub-headline** -- What the offer is and why it matters
4. **Punchy bullet points** -- 5-6 pain points, benefits, or secrets
5. **Social proof** -- Testimonials, logos, numbers, case studies
6. **Clear CTA** -- One single, unmistakable action
7. **Opt-in form** -- Name and email at minimum (every extra field reduces conversions)

### Landing Page Rules

- Remove ALL navigation (no menu, no links away)
- One page, one offer, one action
- Congruent imagery (mockup of HVCO)
- Mobile-optimized mandatory
- Fast load time (under 3 seconds)

### AI-Enhanced Landing Pages

In the AI-native era, landing pages can become conversational:
- AI agent replaces static form with interactive dialogue
- Qualification questions asked one at a time (reduces form abandonment)
- Dynamic content adjusts based on traffic source and referral context
- Real-time personalization based on detected visitor attributes
- Conversational flows gather richer context than forms alone

### Common Phase 3 Mistakes

- Multiple offers or CTAs on one page
- Navigation menus that let visitors leave
- Stock photos instead of relevant imagery
- Clever headlines instead of clear ones
- Not mobile-optimized
- Slow page load times (> 3 seconds)

## Phase 4: The Godfather Strategy (Irresistible Offer)

**"Make them an offer so good they'd feel stupid saying no."**

If your offer does not keep the founder awake at night wondering if it is too generous,
it is not strong enough.

### The 7 Components of a Godfather Offer

```
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                     THE GODFATHER OFFER                               |
|                                                                       |
|  1. RATIONALE        Why are you being so generous?                   |
|     "We're looking for case studies and testimonials"                 |
|     "We're launching in your area and want to prove results"         |
|                                                                       |
|  2. BUILD VALUE      Establish FULL value before price                |
|     List every deliverable with individual market price               |
|     Compare to competitor pricing                                     |
|     Show cost of NOT solving the problem                              |
|                                                                       |
|  3. PRICING          Aggressive introductory offer                    |
|     Loss leader front-end to acquire customer                         |
|     Tiered pricing: Good / Better / Best                              |
|     Charm pricing: $497 not $500                                      |
|                                                                       |
|  4. PAYMENT OPTIONS  Reduce perceived price                           |
|     3-4 installments ($997 becomes "3 payments of $349")              |
|     Small premium for payment plans                                   |
|     Always offer pay-in-full discount alongside                       |
|                                                                       |
|  5. PREMIUMS         Bonuses that tip "maybe" to "yes"                |
|     3-5 named bonuses with specific dollar values                     |
|     Each solves a related but different problem                       |
|     Digital premiums = high value, zero delivery cost                 |
|                                                                       |
|  6. POWER GUARANTEE  Reverse ALL risk to you                          |
|     Standard: 30-day money-back                                       |
|     Double: "I'll refund double your investment"                      |
|     Results: "If you don't get [X], you don't pay"                    |
|     Keep-everything: "Even if you refund, keep the bonuses"           |
|     Conditional: "Do the work. No results? Full refund + $100"        |
|                                                                       |
|  7. SCARCITY         Must be GENUINE (fake = trust destruction)       |
|     Quantity: "Only 50 spots available"                               |
|     Time: "Offer expires Friday at midnight"                          |
|     Bonus: "Order in 24 hours for [bonus]"                            |
|     Price: "Price increases to $997 on Monday"                        |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Example Value Stack

| Component | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Core product/service | $2,000 |
| Bonus 1: Training module | $500 |
| Bonus 2: Templates pack | $300 |
| Bonus 3: Community access | $200 |
| Bonus 4: Strategy call | $500 |
| **Total Value** | **$3,500** |
| **Your Price** | **$497** |
| **Perceived Value Ratio** | **7:1** |

**Rule of thumb:** Total perceived value should be at least 10x the asking price.

### Offer Types in Detail

**Free + Shipping:** Product free, customer pays $7-$12 shipping. Most powerful word
in marketing is "free." Gets customer into ecosystem. Back-end: order bump ($19-$37),
one-click upsell ($47-$97), email sequence to core offer.

**Trial:** Full access for limited time at reduced/zero price. Make trial long enough
for real value. Exceptional onboarding during trial. Clear conversion point at end.

**Assessment / Audit:** Free or low-cost analysis of prospect's current situation.
Provides immediate personalized value. Positions you as expert. Reveals specific
problems you can solve (natural lead-in to paid offer).

### Competitive Differentiation Through Offers

Most businesses compete on product or price. Suby argues compete on the OFFER:

1. **Guarantee differentiation:** Competitors offer 30 days? Offer 365.
2. **Value stack differentiation:** Include bonuses competitors don't.
3. **Risk reversal differentiation:** "We work free until you see results."
4. **Speed differentiation:** "First results in 7 days or less."
5. **Specificity differentiation:** Not "grow your business" but "47 new leads per month."
6. **Mechanism differentiation:** Name your unique process (proprietary method).
7. **Format differentiation:** If everyone sells courses, sell done-for-you.

### Godfather Offer Checklist

- [ ] Solves a specific, painful problem for the Dream Buyer
- [ ] Clear, believable rationale for the generosity
- [ ] Value established before price revealed
- [ ] Total perceived value at least 10x asking price
- [ ] 3-5 compelling named premiums/bonuses
- [ ] Payment plan option available
- [ ] Power Guarantee that reverses all risk
- [ ] Genuine scarcity or urgency mechanism
- [ ] Simple enough to explain in one sentence
- [ ] Landing page follows proven structure
- [ ] Headline tested with 2+ variants
- [ ] Abundant social proof present
- [ ] CTA clear and action-oriented
- [ ] "Would YOU feel stupid saying no?" test passed

## Phase 5: Traffic -- Paid and Organic

Once Dream Buyer is identified, HVCO created, landing page built, and Godfather Offer
crafted, drive traffic.

### Suby's Traffic Philosophy

- Start with paid traffic: fastest way to validate your funnel
- Once funnel is profitable, layer in organic for long-term compounding
- Track every dollar spent and every lead generated
- Kill campaigns that don't convert, scale campaigns that do
- Focus budget on platforms where Dream Buyer spends time
- Retargeting is the cheapest traffic you can buy -- never skip it

### Dream 100 Strategy (Shared with Brunson)

| Step | Timeline | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| 1. Identify | Week 1 | List 100 people/platforms with your Dream Buyer's attention |
| 2. Categorize | Week 1 | Group: influencers, podcasts, blogs, channels, groups, businesses |
| 3. Research | Weeks 1-2 | Understand what each values and needs |
| 4. Give First | Weeks 2-4 | Share content, buy products, leave reviews, comment |
| 5. Outreach | Weeks 4-6 | Personalized contact with specific collaboration proposal |
| 6. Collaborate | Weeks 6-12 | JVs, guest appearances, cross-promotions |
| 7. Nurture | Ongoing | Maintain relationships for mutual benefit |

### Retargeting Strategy

| Audience Segment | Message Angle | Priority |
|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Visited landing page, didn't opt in | Reiterate HVCO value + social proof | High |
| Downloaded HVCO, didn't buy | Testimonials + Godfather Offer reminder | High |
| Watched video > 50% | Case study + direct offer | Medium |
| Cart abandonment | Urgency + guarantee emphasis | Critical |
| Past customers | Upsell / cross-sell next rung | Medium |

## Phase 6: The Magic Lantern Technique (Nurture)

After opt-in, do NOT immediately pitch. Nurture with a sequence of value-driven content.
The content ratio is 80% value, 20% pitch.

### How the Magic Lantern Works

1. **Map the Journey:** Timeline from where customer IS to where they WANT to be
2. **Identify Milestones:** 4-5 milestones they need to reach along the way
3. **Create Content for Each Milestone:**
   - Video/email 1: First quick result
   - Video/email 2: Builds on first result
   - Video/email 3: Positions you as the expert
4. **Include CTA:** At end of each piece, invite next step

### Magic Lantern Sequence Structure

| Email # | Day | Content | Ratio | CTA |
|---------|-----|---------|-------|-----|
| 1 | Day 0 | Deliver HVCO, welcome, set expectations | 100% value | Consume the HVCO |
| 2 | Day 1 | Quick win content (Milestone 1) | 90/10 | Implement the tip |
| 3 | Day 3 | Deeper insight + case study (Milestone 2) | 80/20 | Soft mention of offer |
| 4 | Day 5 | Transformation story (Milestone 3) | 80/20 | Book call / learn more |
| 5 | Day 7 | Present Godfather Offer | 40/60 | Buy / book / start trial |
| 6 | Day 8 | Social proof + FAQ | 50/50 | Reiterate offer |
| 7 | Day 10 | Objection handling | 50/50 | Address specific concerns |
| 8 | Day 12 | Scarcity / last chance | 30/70 | Final deadline push |
| 9 | Day 14 | Final follow-up + alternative offer | 50/50 | Alternative path |

### By Giving Results in Advance You:

- Move prospects closer to their desired outcome
- Build reputation and trust before asking for money
- Reduce suspicion of your business
- Demonstrate competence (show, don't tell)
- Create reciprocity -- they received value and feel inclined to give back

### AI-Enhanced Magic Lantern

Conversational AI transforms the nurture sequence:
- AI monitors engagement (opens, clicks, video completion) and adjusts pace
- Non-engagers get alternative content formats automatically
- High-engagers get accelerated to the offer faster
- AI personalizes content based on quiz/qualification data from Phase 3
- Behavioral scoring triggers human intervention for high-value prospects

### Common Phase 6 Mistakes

- Pitching too early before providing value
- Sending only one email and expecting a sale
- Not including video (builds trust faster than text)
- Content about you instead of about them
- No CTA in each message
- Random sending times without consistency
- Not segmenting based on engagement

## Phase 7: Sales Conversion

Converting nurtured leads into paying customers. Equally about disqualifying wrong
prospects.

### Sales Call Framework

1. **Build Rapport:** Connect on personal level (2-3 minutes)
2. **Diagnose:** Ask questions to deeply understand their situation (10-15 minutes)
3. **Prescribe:** Present solution as logical answer to their specific problems (5-10 min)
4. **Handle Objections:** Address concerns with empathy and evidence (5-10 minutes)
5. **Close:** Ask for commitment directly and confidently (2-3 minutes)
6. **Onboard:** Set clear expectations for next steps (3-5 minutes)

### The Herd Mentality Framework

People follow the crowd. Integrate social proof at EVERY phase:

| Phase | Social Proof Integration |
|-------|------------------------|
| Phase 1 | Use real customer language and stories in buyer research |
| Phase 2 | Include case studies and results in HVCO |
| Phase 3 | Testimonials and trust badges on landing pages |
| Phase 4 | Client results and transformations in Godfather Offer |
| Phase 5 | Social proof in ad copy and creatives |
| Phase 6 | Customer stories in nurture emails |
| Phase 7 | Case studies and references during sales conversations |
| Phase 8 | Success stories to drive referrals and upsells |

### The 17-Step Secret Selling System

Suby's complete copy structure for sales pages and presentations:

1. Call out to audience
2. Demand attention with bold headline
3. Back up the big promise with proof
4. Create irresistible intrigue
5. Shine light on the problem (agitation)
6. Provide the solution
7. Show your credentials
8. Detail the benefits (not features)
9. Stack social proof
10. Make the Godfather Offer
11. Add bonuses
12. Stack total value
13. Reveal the price (after value established)
14. Inject scarcity
15. Give a power guarantee
16. Clear call to action
17. Close with P.S. (warning + reminder)

### Conversion Stats to Remember

- 80% of sales require 5+ touchpoints / follow-ups
- Most salespeople give up after 1-2 attempts
- The fortune is literally in the follow-up
- Disqualifying bad fits is as important as closing good ones

### Suby's Scaling Philosophy

- Find a system that works, then multiply exponentially
- Focus on the metrics, not the tactics

- Build SOPs for every process
- Continuously A/B test and optimize each phase

## Integration: Suby's 8 Phases Mapped to Brunson's Value Ladder

The two systems are complementary. Suby provides the tactical execution engine.

```

Phase 1: Dream Buyer        <-->   Secret Formula Q1-Q2
```

### Where They Diverge

| Dimension | Brunson | Suby |
|-----------|---------|------|

## Real-World Examples: Godfather Offers by Industry

### Example 1: Digital Marketing Agency (B2B)
- **Offer:** "Free 45-minute marketing audit + custom growth plan (valued at $2,500)"
- **Rationale:** "We're expanding into your industry and looking for 5 case studies"
- **Guarantee:** "If you don't find at least 3 immediate growth opportunities, we'll donate $500 to your charity of choice"
- **Scarcity:** "Only 10 audits available per month due to team capacity"

### Example 2: Online Course Creator (B2C)
- **Offer:** "Complete photography masterclass ($1,997 value) for $297 with 365-day guarantee"
- **Premiums:** Lightroom presets ($197), private community ($97/mo free for 3 months), gear guide ($47), weekly Q&A calls ($297)
- **Guarantee:** "Complete 3 modules. If your photos don't improve, full refund + keep everything"

### Example 3: SaaS Company
- **Offer:** "14-day free trial + done-for-you setup ($500 value) + 90-day money-back"
- **Rationale:** "We want you to see results before you invest a dime"
- **Scarcity:** "Done-for-you setup only available for the next 50 signups"

### Example 4: Local Service Business (Dentist)
- **Offer:** "Free comprehensive dental exam + X-rays + whitening consultation ($350 value)"
- **Rationale:** "New patient special -- we want to earn your trust"
- **Guarantee:** "If you're not completely satisfied with your visit, your next cleaning is free"

### Example 5: Coaching/Consulting
- **Offer:** "Strategy session + custom roadmap ($1,500 value) -- no charge, no obligation"
- **Rationale:** "I'm selecting 3 new clients this quarter and want to make sure we're a great fit"
- **Guarantee:** "If the roadmap isn't worth 10x what you'd pay a consultant, I'll send you $100"
- **Scarcity:** "3 spots only -- application closes Friday"
