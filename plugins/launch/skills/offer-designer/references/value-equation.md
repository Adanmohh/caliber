# Alex Hormozi's Value Equation Framework

## Source & Attribution

From "$100M Offers: How to Make Offers So Good People Feel Stupid Saying No" by Alex Hormozi
(Acquisition.com). The Value Equation is the foundational framework for all offer design in the
Hormozi methodology. Every offer audit, redesign, and creation begins here.

---

## The Value Equation

```
                 Dream Outcome  x  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
  Value = -----------------------------------------------------------------------
                      Time Delay  x  Effort & Sacrifice
```

The goal is to make the numerator (what clients WANT) as large as possible and the denominator
(what clients DON'T WANT) as small as possible. The result is perceived value so high that
price becomes irrelevant.

### The Core Insight

Most businesses compete on the numerator -- they make bigger promises. But Hormozi's key insight
is that the denominator is where fortunes are built. Reducing Time Delay and Effort & Sacrifice
is harder to copy, harder to fake, and creates the deepest competitive moats. As Hormozi says:
"The businesses that are worth a fortune spend all their time on the bottom side of the equation."

### Why This Matters for AI Agent Systems

An AI agent system scores offers computationally rather than intuitively. Each variable receives
a numerical score with explicit justification, and the composite Value Score is calculated
mathematically. This removes bias, enables comparative analysis across competitors, and allows
sensitivity modeling (what happens if we improve variable X by 2 points?).

## The Four Variables -- Deep Analysis

### Variable 1: Dream Outcome (Numerator -- MAXIMIZE)

**Definition:** The ideal end-state result the customer desires. Not what the product does, but
the life transformation it enables. The bigger and more emotionally resonant the dream outcome,
the more someone will pay.

**Key Principles:**
- Express the outcome in the CUSTOMER'S language, not yours
- Make it concrete, specific, and measurable -- vague outcomes signal vague expertise
- Attach it to a deep emotional driver (status, health, wealth, relationships, freedom, security)
- The dream outcome is NOT your product -- it is the AFTER state your product creates
- Specificity implies certainty and expertise: "grow your business" vs. "add $50K/month in
  recurring revenue within 90 days"
- The dream outcome must pass the "before/after story" test -- can you describe a vivid
  transformation from current state to dream state?

**Scoring Methodology (1-10):**

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| 1-2 | Generic, vague, incremental improvement. No emotional resonance. Could describe any product in the category. | "Save some time." "Get healthier." "Improve your business." |
| 3-4 | Somewhat specific but common. Not differentiated from competitors. Weak emotional pull. | "Lose weight." "Get more leads." "Learn marketing." |
| 5-6 | Meaningful and specific but not transformational. Moderate emotional resonance. Measurable but not exciting. | "Lose 20 pounds in 6 months." "Get 10 more clients." "Double your email open rates." |
| 7-8 | Highly specific, measurable, and desirable. Strong emotional pull. Clear before/after contrast. Differentiated. | "Add $50K/month in recurring revenue within 90 days." "Drop 4 dress sizes in 12 weeks eating foods you love." |
| 9-10 | Life-changing, identity-level transformation. Extreme specificity. Addresses a deep, burning desire. Impossible to ignore. | "Go from broke personal trainer to gym owner doing $100K/month." "Build a $1M/year business working 20 hours/week from anywhere in the world." |

**Diagnostic Questions:**
1. Does the dream outcome connect to a deep emotional desire (not just a logical benefit)?
2. Is it stated in specific, measurable terms with numbers and timelines?
3. Would the customer describe it EXACTLY this way to a friend over drinks?
4. Does it feel like it belongs in a compelling before/after transformation story?
5. Is it big enough to justify premium pricing?
6. Does it differ meaningfully from what competitors promise?
7. Can you point to someone who has actually achieved this outcome through your offer?

**How to Improve Dream Outcome Score:**

```
IMPROVEMENT DECISION TREE

Is the dream outcome specific and measurable?
  NO --> Add numbers, timelines, and concrete metrics
        "Get more clients" --> "Book 30 qualified sales calls per month"
  YES --> Continue

Does it connect to a deep emotional driver?
  NO --> Map the outcome to status, wealth, health, relationships, freedom, or security
        "Improve SEO rankings" --> "Become the #1 authority in your market so clients
        come to you instead of you chasing them"
  YES --> Continue

Is it stated in the customer's language?
  NO --> Interview 10 customers and use their exact words
        "Optimize your conversion funnel" --> "Get more customers without spending
        more on ads"
  YES --> Continue

Is it differentiated from competitors?
  NO --> Add a unique mechanism, constraint removal, or identity transformation
        "Lose 30 pounds" --> "Lose 30 pounds in 90 days without giving up carbs
        or spending hours in the gym"
  YES --> Score 8+
```

**Common Mistakes:**
1. Confusing FEATURES with OUTCOMES -- "Access to 50 video modules" is a feature; "Master
   Facebook ads in 30 days" is an outcome
2. Using industry jargon instead of customer language -- customers say "get more customers,"
   not "optimize your acquisition funnel"
3. Making the outcome too small to justify the price -- "save 10 minutes a day" does not
   justify $5,000
4. Promising outcomes you cannot prove -- a 10/10 dream outcome with zero proof backfires
   (see Perceived Likelihood)

### Variable 2: Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (Numerator -- MAXIMIZE)

**Definition:** How confident the prospect is that THEY will actually achieve the dream outcome
if they buy. This is not your actual success rate -- it is their BELIEF about their personal
probability of success. A prospect who has tried and failed three times with similar products
has a low perceived likelihood regardless of your actual results.

**Key Principles:**
- Social proof is the strongest driver: testimonials, case studies, before/after results
- Guarantees directly increase perceived likelihood (they signal YOUR confidence)
- Specificity of the process increases confidence (a clear, named methodology feels safer)
- Celebrity or authority endorsements boost perceived likelihood
- Past attempts and failures REDUCE perceived likelihood and must be explicitly overcome
- The prospect is not asking "does this work?" -- they are asking "will this work FOR ME?"
- Perceived likelihood is where TRUST lives in the equation

**Scoring Methodology (1-10):**

| Score | Criteria | Proof Elements Present |
|-------|----------|----------------------|
| 1-2 | No proof whatsoever. Vague promises. Prospect has tried and failed with similar offers before. New or unknown provider. | Zero testimonials, no guarantee, no case studies, no credentials. |
| 3-4 | Some proof but thin. A few testimonials that are generic or from dissimilar avatars. No guarantee. | 1-5 generic testimonials, basic credentials, some experience. |
| 5-6 | Moderate proof. Reasonable number of testimonials from relevant avatars. Some form of guarantee. Credible methodology. | 10-20 testimonials, some case studies, basic guarantee, named methodology. |
| 7-8 | Strong proof. Extensive case studies with specific results from avatars matching the prospect. Clear methodology. Strong guarantee. Authority positioning. | 20-50+ testimonials with specific numbers, detailed case studies, strong guarantee, published methodology, media appearances. |
| 9-10 | Overwhelming proof. Near-universal success stories. Performance-based pricing. Celebrity endorsements. Industry recognition. Prospect cannot find a reason to doubt. | 100+ testimonials, video case studies, performance guarantee, celebrity/authority endorsements, awards, media coverage, academic validation. |

**Diagnostic Questions:**
1. How many testimonials/case studies do you have? (Quantity matters)
2. Are the testimonials from people SIMILAR to the prospect? (Avatar match matters more than
   quantity)
3. Do testimonials include SPECIFIC results with numbers? ("I made $47K in 60 days" beats
   "Great program!")
4. Do you have a clear, step-by-step methodology the prospect can SEE before buying?
5. Is there a guarantee that reduces perceived risk?
6. Has the prospect tried and failed with competitors? (If yes, you must address this directly)
7. Do you have any third-party validation (media, awards, certifications)?
8. Can the prospect talk to existing customers before buying?

**Perceived Likelihood Boosters -- Ranked by Impact:**

```
IMPACT RANKING (Highest to Lowest)

1. Performance-based pricing       -- "We only get paid when you get results"
   Impact: Extreme                    Eliminates risk entirely
   Cost: Variable                     Cash flow unpredictability

2. Specific, avatar-matched        -- Case studies with exact numbers from people
   case studies                       who look/sound like the prospect
   Impact: Very High                  "She was a solo consultant doing $8K/month
   Cost: Low (time to collect)        just like me, and now she does $45K/month"

3. Conditional guarantee            -- "Do the work, get the result, or get your
   Impact: High                       money back"
   Cost: Low (most people             Signals confidence + filters tire-kickers
   don't claim)

4. Named, visible methodology      -- A proprietary system with steps they can see
   Impact: High                       "The 5-Phase Revenue Acceleration System"
   Cost: Zero (you already            Makes the path feel clear and proven
   have a process)

5. Unconditional guarantee          -- "Try it for 30 days, refund for any reason"
   Impact: High                       Maximum risk removal but some abuse risk
   Cost: Moderate (refund rate)

6. Volume of social proof           -- Sheer number of positive testimonials
   Impact: Moderate-High              200 reviews > 20 reviews > 2 reviews
   Cost: Low (systematic
   collection)

7. Third-party validation           -- Media features, awards, certifications
   Impact: Moderate                   "As seen in Forbes," "Award-winning"
   Cost: Variable

8. Community access before          -- Let prospects see the community, talk to
   purchase                           members, attend a free session
   Impact: Moderate                   Reduces "what if it sucks?" fear
   Cost: Low
```

**Common Mistakes:**
1. Assuming your product's actual effectiveness equals the prospect's PERCEIVED likelihood --
   perception is reality in this equation
2. Having testimonials from the wrong avatar -- a Fortune 500 case study does not help a
   solopreneur believe THEY can succeed
3. Hiding your methodology -- if the prospect cannot see the path, they assume there is no path
4. No guarantee when competitors offer one -- instant credibility gap
5. Not addressing past failures -- if the prospect has been burned before, you MUST address
   it directly: "I know you've tried X before and it didn't work. Here's why this is different."

### Variable 3: Time Delay (Denominator -- MINIMIZE)

**Definition:** The time between the moment of purchase and the realization of the dream outcome.
The longer the delay, the lower the perceived value. Time kills deals. Time kills excitement.
Time kills commitment.

**Key Principles:**
- Immediate results are exponentially more valuable than delayed results
- Even the PERCEPTION of quick results matters -- engineer quick wins
- "Time to first value" is critical -- what happens in the first 24-48 hours?
- Break the journey into milestones so they experience progress EARLY
- Ongoing subscription models suffer here unless early wins are engineered
- The prospect discounts future value heavily -- a result in 7 days feels 10x more valuable
  than the same result in 6 months
- Quick wins also boost Perceived Likelihood (compound effect)

**Scoring Methodology (1-10):**

| Score | Criteria | Typical Timeframes |
|-------|----------|--------------------|
| 1-2 | No timeline communicated. Results take 6-12+ months. No interim milestones. Prospect has no idea when to expect anything. | "Results vary." "It takes time." No milestones. |
| 3-4 | Results in 3-6 months. Some vague milestones. First meaningful result is weeks away. | "You'll start seeing traction in a few months." |
| 5-6 | Results in 1-3 months. Clear milestones exist. Some early wins possible in first 1-2 weeks. | "First results in 2-4 weeks, full results in 90 days." |
| 7-8 | Results in 1-4 weeks. Clear quick wins in first 48 hours. Tangible progress visible immediately. Strong milestone structure. | "See your first lead within 48 hours. Full system live in 2 weeks." |
| 9-10 | Instant or near-instant results. Immediate transformation visible. The product delivers value the moment it is consumed/activated. | "Results in minutes." "Instant access, immediate impact." Software tools, templates, done-for-you services with same-day delivery. |

**Diagnostic Questions:**
1. How long until the customer sees their FIRST tangible result? (Not the full outcome -- the
   first sign of progress)
2. Can you engineer a quick win in the first 24-48 hours?
3. Are there intermediate milestones that create a sense of momentum?
4. Is the timeline clearly communicated and believable?
5. Can you compress the timeline without sacrificing quality?
6. What can the customer see, feel, or measure on Day 1?
7. Does onboarding create an immediate sense of progress or an immediate sense of overwhelm?

**Time Delay Reduction Strategies:**

```
STRATEGY                          IMPACT    COST     EXAMPLE
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Quick-start onboarding            High      Low      "Complete your profile and see your
                                                     first recommendation in 5 minutes"

Done-for-you setup                High      Medium   "We set everything up for you.
                                                     You just approve and launch."

Template/swipe file library       High      Low      "Use our proven templates -- don't
                                                     start from scratch"

Day-1 deliverable                 High      Low      "Within 24 hours of joining, you
                                                     receive your custom plan"

Progress dashboard                Medium    Medium   "Watch your metrics improve in
                                                     real-time on your dashboard"

Milestone celebrations            Medium    Low      "Week 1 checkpoint: you've completed
                                                     Phase 1 -- here's what's next"

Fast-track option                 Medium    High     "Add the Fast-Track Package and
                                                     compress 90 days into 30"

Interim result delivery           High      Medium   "First batch of leads delivered
                                                     within 48 hours of onboarding"
```

**Common Mistakes:**
1. No timeline at all -- if you do not tell the prospect when to expect results, they assume
   "probably never"
2. Promising only the END result without intermediate milestones -- "You'll have a 6-figure
   business in 12 months" with nothing in between is a motivation killer
3. Confusing time to DELIVERY with time to RESULTS -- delivering the course in 24 hours is
   not the same as the customer getting results in 24 hours
4. Not engineering quick wins -- even if the full result takes months, the FIRST result should
   happen fast. Quick wins sustain motivation and reduce refund rates.
5. Overcomplicating onboarding -- if the first 48 hours are spent watching training videos
   instead of DOING something that creates a result, you lose them

### Variable 4: Effort & Sacrifice (Denominator -- MINIMIZE)

**Definition:** The perceived amount of work, complexity, inconvenience, and things-given-up
required from the customer to achieve the result. Effort is what they must DO. Sacrifice is
what they must GIVE UP.

**Key Principles:**
- "Done for you" beats "done with you" beats "do it yourself" -- always
- Complexity is the enemy -- every additional step reduces conversion AND completion
- Cognitive load matters as much as physical effort -- confusion is effort
- Sacrifice includes giving up comfort, habits, time, relationships, or identity
- Each additional step in a process creates a dropout point
- The goal is to make the customer's path feel like a gentle downhill walk, not a mountain climb
- Your job is to do MORE so the customer does LESS

**Scoring Methodology (1-10):**

| Score | Criteria | Customer Experience |
|-------|----------|---------------------|
| 1-2 | Complex, multi-step process requiring major lifestyle changes. Steep learning curve. Significant sacrifice. Customer must fundamentally change who they are. | "Learn our proprietary software, restructure your team, change your daily routine, study 20 hours of material, pass a certification exam." |
| 3-4 | Considerable effort required. New skills to learn. Moderate lifestyle adjustments. Multiple systems to manage. | "Watch 10 modules, complete weekly homework, attend 3 calls/week, implement between sessions." |
| 5-6 | Moderate effort. Some new skills needed but manageable. Reasonable workload. Some sacrifice but not painful. | "Follow the system, attend weekly calls, spend 3-5 hours/week on implementation." |
| 7-8 | Simple process. Minimal learning curve. Mostly done-for-you or done-with-you. Little sacrifice. Clear, few steps. | "Show up to 2 calls/month, approve the work we do for you, answer a few questions." |
| 9-10 | Zero or near-zero effort. Fully done-for-you. No sacrifice needed. The "magic pill." Customer does almost nothing and gets the result. | "We handle everything. You just review the results." Software that works automatically. One-click solutions. |

**Diagnostic Questions:**
1. How many steps does the customer need to take to get the result?
2. What must they give up or change in their daily routine?
3. Can any customer steps be eliminated, automated, or done for them?
4. Is the process simple enough to explain in one sentence?
5. What is the cognitive load required? (How much do they need to learn/understand?)
6. Are there technical barriers? (Software to learn, accounts to create, integrations to set up)
7. Would a busy, non-technical person feel overwhelmed by the process?

**Effort & Sacrifice Reduction Strategies:**

```
STRATEGY                          IMPACT    COST     EXAMPLE
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Done-for-you service              Extreme   High     "We build your entire funnel.
                                                     You approve and launch."

Templates and swipe files         High      Low      "Fill in the blanks. Don't
                                                     start from scratch."

Automation                        High      Medium   "Our system automatically sends
                                                     follow-ups. You do nothing."

Concierge onboarding              High      Medium   "Your dedicated onboarding
                                                     specialist handles setup for you."

Simplified process                High      Low      Reduce 12 steps to 3 steps.
                                                     Remove unnecessary complexity.

Decision elimination              Medium    Low      "We've already chosen the best
                                                     option for you. Just say yes/no."

Habit integration                 Medium    Low      "Fits into your existing routine.
                                                     No lifestyle changes required."

Tech barrier removal              Medium    Medium   "No software to learn. We use
                                                     tools you already know."

Sacrifice reframing               Medium    Low      "You don't give up pizza. You eat
                                                     pizza AND lose weight."
```

**Common Mistakes:**
1. Confusing YOUR effort with THEIR effort -- the effort variable is about what the CUSTOMER
   does, not what you do behind the scenes. You should be doing more so they do less.
2. Adding features that increase complexity -- more is not better if more means more effort
   for the customer
3. Requiring the customer to learn your system -- if your solution requires a learning curve,
   that is effort. Reduce or eliminate it.
4. Not addressing sacrifice explicitly -- if your program requires them to give up something
   (time, money, comfort), address it in the offer: "without giving up X"
5. Treating all customers as equally capable -- a technical founder and a non-technical
   small business owner have very different effort thresholds

## How to Score an Offer Using the Value Equation

### Step 1: Define the Avatar

Before scoring, get crystal clear on WHO the offer is for. The same offer scores differently
for different avatars. A complex software tool might score 8/10 on Effort for a developer but
2/10 for a non-technical business owner.

```
Avatar Definition Checklist:
- Who specifically is this for? (Demographics + psychographics)
- What is their current situation? (Income, business stage, pain level)
- What have they tried before? (Previous solutions, failures, frustrations)
- What is their technical/skill level? (Determines effort scoring)
- What is their time availability? (Determines time delay sensitivity)
- What are they willing to sacrifice? (Budget, time, comfort, habits)
```

### Step 2: Score Each Variable (1-10)

Rate each variable independently using the scoring criteria above. Score from the AVATAR'S
perspective, not your own.

**Critical rule:** If you find yourself scoring your own offer, get external input. Founders
consistently over-score Dream Outcome and under-score Effort because they see their product
through their own lens, not the customer's.

### Step 3: Calculate the Value Score

```
Value Score = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / ((11 - Time Delay) x (11 - Effort))

SCORING REMINDER:
  - Time Delay and Effort are scored 1-10 where 10 = MINIMAL delay/effort (best)
    and 1 = MAXIMUM delay/effort (worst)
  - The formula inverts these scores: (11 - score) converts them so that
    high scores (low delay/effort) produce a SMALL denominator = HIGH value
    low scores (high delay/effort) produce a LARGE denominator = LOW value

IMPORTANT: The denominator is SMALLEST when Time Delay and Effort scores are
HIGHEST (i.e., minimal delay and minimal effort), which correctly produces the
highest Value Scores for the best offers.

Example: Dream Outcome = 8, Perceived Likelihood = 7, Time Delay = 6, Effort = 5
Value Score = (8 x 7) / ((11-6) x (11-5)) = 56 / (5 x 6) = 56 / 30 = 1.87
```

### Step 4: Interpret the Score

```
VALUE SCORE INTERPRETATION TABLE

Score Range     Rating              What It Means
-----------     ------              -------------
< 0.5           CRITICALLY WEAK     Major redesign needed. Offer is likely losing
                                    to "do nothing" as an alternative.

0.5 - 2.0       BELOW AVERAGE       Significant gaps exist. Offer converts but
                                    poorly. Competing on price, not value.

2.0 - 5.0       AVERAGE             Competitive but not compelling. In the middle
                                    of the market. Vulnerable to competitors.

5.0 - 10.0      STRONG              Differentiated and attractive. Above-market
                                    conversion rates. Can charge premium prices.

10.0 - 20.0     EXCELLENT           People feel compelled to buy. Strong word of
                                    mouth. High conversion, low refunds.

20.0+           GRAND SLAM          "Stupid saying no" territory. Category of one.
                                    Can charge the highest prices in the market.
```

### Step 5: Identify the Weakest Variable

The weakest variable is your biggest lever for improvement. Improving a 3 to a 7 has a massive
compounding effect on the total score.

**Priority Framework for Improvement:**

```
PRIORITY DECISION TREE

1. Is any variable scored 1-3?
   YES --> Fix this FIRST. Scores of 1-3 are deal-killers that suppress
           the entire equation regardless of other strengths.
   NO  --> Continue

2. Which variable is easiest to improve by 2+ points?
   Identify the variable where a concrete, low-cost action would produce
   the biggest score jump. Often this is Perceived Likelihood (add
   testimonials, add a guarantee) or Time Delay (engineer a quick win).

3. Focus on the denominator.
   If numerator variables are already 7+, shift focus to reducing Time Delay
   and Effort & Sacrifice. This is where Hormozi says fortunes are built
   because denominator improvements are harder to copy.

4. Model the impact.
   Calculate the Value Score with each variable improved by 2 points.
   The variable that produces the largest delta is your primary lever.
```

### Step 6: Sensitivity Analysis (AI Agent Advantage)

An AI agent system can model multiple improvement scenarios simultaneously:

```
SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS TEMPLATE

Current Score: (DO x PL) / ((11-TD) x (11-ES)) = Value Score

Scenario A: Improve Dream Outcome by 2
  New Score: ((DO+2) x PL) / ((11-TD) x (11-ES)) = [Calculate] (Delta: +[X]%)

Scenario B: Improve Perceived Likelihood by 2
  New Score: (DO x (PL+2)) / ((11-TD) x (11-ES)) = [Calculate] (Delta: +[X]%)

Scenario C: Improve Time Delay by 2 (denominator shrinks)
  New Score: (DO x PL) / ((11-(TD+2)) x (11-ES)) = [Calculate] (Delta: +[X]%)

Scenario D: Improve Effort & Sacrifice by 2 (denominator shrinks)
  New Score: (DO x PL) / ((11-TD) x (11-(ES+2))) = [Calculate] (Delta: +[X]%)

Scenario E: Improve weakest two variables by 1 each
  New Score: [Calculate] (Delta: +[X]%)

PRIMARY LEVER: Scenario [X] produces the largest delta (+[Y]%)
SECONDARY LEVER: Scenario [X] produces the second largest delta (+[Y]%)
```

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: SaaS Lead Generation Tool

**Avatar:** B2B SaaS founder, $500K-$2M ARR, 2-10 employees, doing their own outbound sales,
wants to scale without hiring a sales team.

**Before Optimization:**

| Variable | Score | Assessment |
|----------|-------|------------|
| Dream Outcome | 5 | "Get more leads" -- generic, not specific, no numbers |
| Perceived Likelihood | 3 | New product, 4 generic testimonials, no guarantee |
| Time Delay | 4 | Takes 2-3 months to see pipeline impact, no quick wins |
| Effort & Sacrifice | 4 | Complex setup, CRM integration, team training needed |

**Value Score:** (5 x 3) / ((11-4) x (11-4)) = 15 / (7 x 7) = 15 / 49 = 0.31 -- CRITICALLY WEAK

**After Optimization:**

| Variable | Score | Assessment |
|----------|-------|------------|
| Dream Outcome | 9 | "Book 30+ qualified sales calls per month on autopilot without hiring a sales team" |
| Perceived Likelihood | 8 | 47 case studies with specific ROI numbers, conditional guarantee: "30 qualified calls in 60 days or we work free until you do" |
| Time Delay | 8 | "First qualified lead within 48 hours of setup. Full pipeline in 2 weeks." |
| Effort & Sacrifice | 8 | "We set up everything. You just show up to calls. 15-minute onboarding, we handle the rest." |

**Value Score:** (9 x 8) / ((11-8) x (11-8)) = 72 / (3 x 3) = 72 / 9 = 8.00 -- STRONG

**What Changed:**
- Dream Outcome: Specific result + timeline + objection removal ("without hiring")
- Perceived Likelihood: 4 testimonials --> 47 case studies + conditional guarantee
- Time Delay: "2-3 months" --> "48 hours to first result"
- Effort: "Complex setup" --> "We handle everything, 15-minute onboarding"

## Competitive Value Equation Comparison Framework

An AI agent system should score not just the client's offer, but also the top competitor offers
and the "do nothing" alternative. This creates a comparative matrix:

```
COMPETITIVE VALUE EQUATION MATRIX

                    Your Offer    Competitor A    Competitor B    Do Nothing
                    ----------    ------------    ------------    ----------
Dream Outcome       [score]       [score]         [score]         N/A
Perceived Like.     [score]       [score]         [score]         N/A
Time Delay           [score]       [score]         [score]         N/A
Effort & Sacrifice   [score]       [score]         [score]         10
                    ----------    ------------    ------------    ----------
Value Score          (DO x PL) /  (DO x PL) /     (DO x PL) /    N/A
                    ((11-TD) x   ((11-TD) x      ((11-TD) x
                     (11-ES))     (11-ES))        (11-ES))

GAPS IDENTIFIED:
- Your offer beats competitors on: [Variables]
- Your offer loses to competitors on: [Variables]
- Your offer loses to "do nothing" on: [Variables -- usually Effort & Sacrifice]
- Primary differentiation opportunity: [Variable where gap is largest]
```

**The "Do Nothing" Competitor:**
The most dangerous competitor is always "do nothing." "Do nothing" scores a perfect 10 on
Effort & Sacrifice (zero effort, zero sacrifice) and a perfect 10 on Time Delay (no time
invested). Your offer must make the Dream Outcome compelling enough and the Perceived
Likelihood high enough to overcome the denominator advantage of inaction.

## The Value Equation Audit Checklist

Use this checklist to validate a complete Value Equation audit:

```
PRE-AUDIT
[ ] Avatar is defined with specificity (demographics + psychographics + history)
[ ] The offer being audited is clearly stated (what, for whom, at what price)
[ ] Competitor offers have been researched (real-time research, not assumptions)

DREAM OUTCOME
[ ] Outcome is stated in the customer's language (validated, not assumed)
[ ] Outcome is specific and measurable (numbers, timelines, concrete results)
[ ] Outcome connects to a deep emotional driver
[ ] Outcome is differentiated from competitor promises
[ ] Score is justified with explicit reasoning
[ ] At least one specific improvement recommendation is provided

PERCEIVED LIKELIHOOD
[ ] Number and quality of testimonials/case studies is assessed
[ ] Avatar match of proof elements is evaluated
[ ] Guarantee presence and strength is assessed
[ ] Methodology visibility is evaluated
[ ] Past failure history of the avatar is considered
[ ] Score is justified with explicit reasoning
[ ] At least one specific improvement recommendation is provided

TIME DELAY
[ ] Time to first result is identified (not time to full result)
[ ] Quick win within 48 hours is assessed (present or absent)
[ ] Milestone structure is evaluated
[ ] Onboarding experience is assessed for speed
[ ] Score is justified with explicit reasoning
[ ] At least one specific improvement recommendation is provided

EFFORT & SACRIFICE
[ ] Number of steps customer must take is counted
[ ] Cognitive load is assessed (how much must they learn?)
[ ] Sacrifice requirements are identified (what must they give up?)
[ ] Done-for-you vs. DIY ratio is evaluated
[ ] Technical barriers are identified
[ ] Score is justified with explicit reasoning
[ ] At least one specific improvement recommendation is provided

POST-SCORING
[ ] Value Score is calculated correctly
[ ] Score is interpreted using the rating table
[ ] Weakest variable is identified with improvement priority
[ ] Sensitivity analysis is performed (impact of +2 on each variable)
[ ] Competitive comparison is completed (vs. top 2 competitors + do nothing)
[ ] Specific, actionable improvement recommendations are provided for each variable
```
