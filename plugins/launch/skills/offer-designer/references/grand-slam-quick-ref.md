# Grand Slam Offer — Quick Reference

> This is the working reference for the Grand Slam Offer framework. For full theory, worked
> examples, and deep guidance on each step, see `grand-slam-offer.md`.

---

## Assembly Line (8 Steps)

```
Step 1: DREAM OUTCOME → Step 2: PROBLEMS → Step 3: DELIVERY VEHICLES → Step 4: TRIM & STACK
Step 5: ENHANCERS → Step 6: NAMING → Step 7: PRICING → Step 8: HANDOFF
```

---

## Step 1: Dream Outcome

**Formula:** `[Specific Result] + [In Timeframe] + [Without Key Sacrifice]`

| Criteria | Pass | Fail |
|----------|------|------|
| Specific & measurable | "Add $50K/month in revenue" | "Grow your business" |
| Customer's language | Words from interviews/reviews | Industry jargon |
| Emotional driver | Status, freedom, security, identity | Logical feature list |
| Objection removal | "without cold calling" | No sacrifice addressed |
| Before/after contrast | Vivid transformation story | Vague improvement |

---

## Step 2: Problem/Solution Map

**4 Problem Categories:**

| Category | When | Key Question |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Before-Purchase | Pre-buy barriers | "Why don't they buy?" |
| During-Process | Fulfillment friction | "Why do they quit?" |
| After-Completion | Outcome sustainability | "Why don't results stick?" |
| Meta-Problems | Problems from solutions | "What new problems does our solution create?" |

**Target:** 30-50 problems across all 4 categories. Every problem gets a solution.

---

## Step 3: Delivery Vehicles (The Delivery Cube)

**3 Dimensions, 2 Options Each = 8 Combinations:**

| Dimension | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Ratio | 1:1 (high touch) vs 1:Many (scalable) |
| Effort | DFY (done-for-you) vs DIY (do-it-yourself) |
| Support | In-person vs Virtual |

**Decision Framework:**

| Factor | Choose High-Touch | Choose Scalable |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| Price point | > $3,000 | < $1,000 |
| Complexity | High | Low |
| Customization | Essential | Nice-to-have |
| Scale goal | Boutique | Mass market |

---

## Step 4: Trim & Stack

**Trim Rule:** Remove anything where Cost-to-Deliver > Value-to-Customer.

**Stack Template:**

| Component | Description | Value | Cost | Keep? |
|-----------|-------------|-------|------|-------|
| Core Offer | [Main deliverable] | $X | $Y | Yes |
| Component 2 | [Description] | $X | $Y | Score |
| Component 3 | [Description] | $X | $Y | Score |

**Value Assignment:** Price what it would cost to get the same result elsewhere (agency, hire, consultant, DIY time cost).

**Target:** Total perceived value ≥ 10x price.

## Step 5: Enhancers

### 5a: Scarcity (Limit Supply)

| Type | Mechanism | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Physical | Limited inventory, materials | Products |
| Analytical | Limited capacity to serve | Services, coaching |
| Personal | Limited time/bandwidth | High-ticket, 1:1 |
| Cohort | Limited seats per group | Programs, courses |

**Rule:** Must be REAL with a logical reason. Never fake it.

### 5b: Urgency (Limit Time)

| Type | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| Rolling | X days from prospect's first contact |
| Cohort | Fixed start dates with enrollment deadlines |
| Seasonal | Tied to external events or business cycles |
| Promotional | Limited-time pricing with hard cutoff |

**Rule:** Every deadline must be enforced. One broken deadline destroys all future urgency.

### 5c: Bonuses

**Strategic Bonus Criteria:**

| Criterion | Good Bonus | Bad Bonus |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Solves specific objection | "Restaurant Guide" for diet program | Generic "resource pack" |
| High perceived value | Addresses a known, named pain | Nice-to-have extra |
| Low delivery cost | Digital, templated, or one-time creation | Custom, labor-intensive |
| Complements core offer | Removes a gap the core doesn't cover | Duplicates core content |

**Target:** 3-5 bonuses, each mapped to a specific objection or problem.

### 5d: Guarantees

**Decision Tree:**

```
Is your refund rate currently < 10%?
  YES → Unconditional guarantee (maximize conversion)
  NO  → Fix delivery first, then guarantee

Can you guarantee a specific, measurable result?
  YES → Conditional guarantee ("X result or Y remedy")
  NO  → Unconditional or anti-guarantee

Is your product high-ticket (> $2,000)?
  YES → Conditional + "we work until you succeed" (strongest conversion driver)
  NO  → Unconditional 30-60 day works well
```

**Guarantee Types:** Unconditional | Conditional | Performance-Based | Anti-Guarantee

## Step 6: Naming — MAGIC Formula

| Letter | Element | Question |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **M** | Magnet | What's the magnetic reason to pay attention? |
| **A** | Avatar | Who specifically is this for? |
| **G** | Goal | What result will they achieve? |
| **I** | Interval | In what timeframe? |
| **C** | Container | What type of thing is it? (System, Blueprint, Program, etc.) |

**Scoring:** 1-5 per element. Target ≥ 15/25. Use 3-5 of the 5 elements.

**Container Words (ranked):** Challenge > Blueprint > System > Accelerator > Program > Course

---

## Step 7: Pricing

**Core Rules:**

| Rule | Principle |
|------|-----------|
| Value-based | Price on outcome value, not delivery cost |
| 10x ratio | Perceived value must be ≥ 10x price |
| Test upward | Raise until conversion drops below target |
| Anchor high | Show total value before revealing price |
| Multiple options | Offer 2-3 tiers (but recommend one) |

**Price Anchoring:** `Total Value ($X) → Your Investment ($Y) → Savings ($X-Y)`

## 10 Fatal Errors (Quick Check)

| # | Error | Fix |
|---|-------|-----|
| 1 | Creating in isolation | Interview 10-20 customers first |
| 2 | Too many components | Keep core to 3-5, layer bonuses |
| 3 | Fake scarcity/urgency | Real mechanisms only, enforce all deadlines |
| 4 | Weak/missing guarantee | Use the decision tree above |
| 5 | Pricing too low | Test upward aggressively |
| 6 | No visual value stack | Break into named, valued components |
| 7 | Generic naming | Apply MAGIC formula, test 3-5 options |
| 8 | Solving wrong problems | Research real customer problems |
| 9 | No quick wins | Engineer Day-1 deliverable |
| 10 | Not testing | Iterate names, prices, guarantees, bonuses |

## Agent Workflow

```
1. RESEARCH    → Competitor offers, customer language, market pricing
2. DIAGNOSTIC  → Value Equation scores + Grand Slam Scorecard
3. DESIGN      → Problems → Solutions → Delivery → Trim/Stack → Enhancers → Name → Price
4. VARIATIONS  → A: Max Value | B: Max Margin | C: Max Conversion | D: Recommended Blend
5. HANDOFF     → Copywriter (copy), Funnel Architect (implementation), Growth Auditor (strategy)
```
