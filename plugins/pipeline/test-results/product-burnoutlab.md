**1.1 Company Mission**
Eliminate chronic burnout for knowledge workers through science-based, AI-personalized recovery programs.

**1.2 Company Strategy Summary**
BurnoutLab fills the gap between expensive executive coaching ($300+/mo) and ineffective wellness apps ($6/mo) by combining clinical frameworks (Maslach Burnout Inventory, positive psychology) with AI personalization. The entry point is structured courses ($197-$497) that convert to an ongoing AI tools subscription ($29/mo).

**1.3 Market Context**

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Market Maturity | Growing -- burnout recognized as occupational phenomenon by WHO (2019); corporate wellness market projected ~$100B+ |
| TAM | $10B+ addressable within knowledge worker burnout recovery (tech, healthcare, finance) |
| Growth Rate | Estimated 15-20% CAGR in digital mental health |
| Key Trends | 1) Post-pandemic burnout normalization; 2) AI-powered mental health tools gaining trust; 3) Employer willingness to fund recovery |

**1.4 Competitive Landscape**

| Competitor | Positioning | Key Advantage |
|-----------|------------|--------------|
| BetterHelp / Talkspace | Therapy-on-demand | Licensed therapists, insurance coverage |
| Calm / Headspace | Meditation & wellness apps | Brand recognition, massive user base, low price ($6/mo) |
| Executive coaches | 1:1 burnout coaching | Deep personalization, accountability |
| Resilience programs (corporate) | Employer-provided wellness | Paid by employer, integrated into HR |

**Our Differentiation:** Science-based structured programs (not generic meditation) + AI personalization (not one-size-fits-all) at an accessible price point ($29/mo ongoing). The combination of clinical rigor + AI + structured curriculum does not exist in the market today.

### SECTION 2: PRODUCT VISION

**2.1 Vision Statement (3-Year Horizon)**

In 3 years, knowledge workers experiencing chronic burnout will recover in 90 days instead of 12+ months because BurnoutLab provides a science-based, AI-personalized recovery system that adapts to their specific burnout profile. This matters because burnout destroys careers, relationships, and health -- and current solutions are either too expensive, too generic, or too shallow.

**2.2 Product Principles**

| Principle | Statement | We DO | We DON'T |
|-----------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Science First | Every recommendation is grounded in clinical research | Use Maslach, positive psychology, CBT frameworks with citations | Offer unvalidated "tips" or generic wellness advice |
| Progress Over Perfection | Recovery is a journey; show users they're moving forward | Track and visualize micro-progress, celebrate small wins | Demand adherence to rigid programs that discourage users |
| Human + AI | AI amplifies human capability; it does not replace human connection | Use AI for personalization, assessment, daily coaching prompts | Claim AI replaces therapy or professional mental health care |

1. PROBLEM
------------------------------------------------------------

### What is the pain?
Knowledge workers suspect they are burned out but have no reliable,
specific way to assess their condition. Generic online quizzes produce
vague results ("You might be stressed!") with no actionable next steps.
Users are left knowing they feel terrible but having no clarity on the
severity, type, or specific recovery path for their burnout. They
either do nothing (and get worse) or spend $300+/mo on coaching they
may not need.

### Who feels it?
Tech professionals (engineers, PMs, designers), healthcare workers,
and finance professionals working 50+ hour weeks. They are high-
achievers who have noticed declining performance, increasing cynicism,
and persistent exhaustion over 3+ months.

### Evidence
- Market signal: "am I burned out" Google search volume has grown 3x
  since 2020 (Google Trends data)

4. RABBIT HOLES
------------------------------------------------------------

### Rabbit Hole 1: Burnout Type Taxonomy
- **Risk:** Designing the "burnout type" labels and mapping scoring
  thresholds to types could become an unbounded research project
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Impact if hit:** 1-2 weeks of taxonomy design and edge-case scoring
- **Mitigation:** Pre-define 4-6 burnout types before development
  starts. Use concierge MVP data + Maslach research to set thresholds.
  Ship with initial taxonomy; refine in v2 based on data.

### Rabbit Hole 2: Personalized Recovery Recommendations
- **Risk:** Generating truly personalized recommendations for each
  burnout profile combination could require complex decision trees
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Impact if hit:** 2+ weeks building recommendation engine
- **Mitigation:** v1 uses template-based recommendations. Each burnout
  type maps to a pre-written set of 3-5 recommendations. No AI/LLM
  generation in v1. Maximum 6 types x 5 recs = 30 pre-written items.

### Rabbit Hole 3: Email Deliverability
- **Risk:** Transactional emails with results could land in spam,
  breaking the core flow
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Impact if hit:** Lost leads; broken conversion flow
- **Mitigation:** Results are shown immediately on the results page
  (not email-dependent). Email is a backup/follow-up channel, not
  the primary delivery mechanism. Use established ESP (e.g., Resend,
  SendGrid) for deliverability.

### Rabbit Hole 4: Clinical/Legal Liability
- **Risk:** Burnout assessment could be perceived as medical diagnosis
- **Likelihood:** Low
- **Impact if hit:** Legal exposure; trust damage
- **Mitigation:** Clear disclaimers throughout: "This is a self-
  assessment tool, not a clinical diagnosis. If you are in crisis,
  contact a healthcare professional." Position as educational, not
  clinical. Do not use the word "diagnosis."

### SECTION 5: DESIGN VARIATIONS

**Variation A: "Clinical Clarity"**
- Approach: Clean, data-forward design emphasizing the scientific foundation. Dimension scores are prominent. Language is precise and professional.
- Strengths: Maximum credibility; appeals to analytical users (engineers, finance)
- Weaknesses: Could feel cold; may increase anxiety for emotionally vulnerable users
- Best For: Users who value data and rigor over warmth

**Variation B: "Warm Recovery" (Recommended)**
- Approach: Warm, supportive design that leads with the human experience. Burnout type is the hero element. Scores are present but secondary to narrative.
- Strengths: Emotionally safe; validates before diagnosing; recovery-focused framing reduces anxiety
- Weaknesses: May feel less "scientific" to data-driven users
- Best For: Users experiencing vulnerability who need emotional safety first

**Variation C: "Interactive Journey"**
- Approach: Highly interactive with animations, transitions, and gamification elements (progress celebrations, animated score reveals)
- Strengths: Engaging; reduces quiz fatigue; memorable experience
- Weaknesses: Higher development cost; accessibility challenges with animations; could feel trivializing for a serious topic
- Best For: Users comfortable with modern web interactions
