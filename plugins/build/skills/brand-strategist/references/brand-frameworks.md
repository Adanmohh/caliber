# Brand Strategy Frameworks

## Marty Neumeier: Brand Gap (5 Disciplines)

The brand gap is the distance between business strategy and customer experience. Close it with 5 disciplines:

### 1. Differentiate
- **The Onliness Test:** "Our [offering] is the only [category] that [differentiator]"
- If you can't complete this sentence compellingly, you don't have a brand
- Differentiation ≠ better. Differentiation = different
- Zag when others zig — find the white space

### 2. Collaborate
- Brand building requires cross-functional alignment
- In AI era: alignment between human team AND AI agents
- Brand voice must be consistent whether human or AI speaks
- Design system = brand's shared language

### 3. Innovate
- Innovation is brand fuel — brands that stop innovating die
- Focus on "good different" not "bad different"
- Test: Would removing this feature/trait make customers care?
- AI-era: AI capabilities ARE brand differentiators (speed, personalization, availability)

### 4. Validate
- Test brand concepts with real customers before committing
- Name test: Does it pass the "Google test"? (unique, spellable, pronounceable)
- Concept test: Show brand elements to target segment, measure recall and emotion
- In pipeline: leverage Experiment Designer validation data

### 5. Cultivate
- Brand is a living system, not a document
- Brand guidelines must be actionable (tokens + voice guide), not aspirational
- Every touchpoint reinforces or erodes brand
- AI era: every AI interaction IS a brand touchpoint

## Marty Neumeier: Zag (17-Step Process, Condensed)

### Phase 1: Find Your Zag
1. **Who are you?** — Core identity, mission, values
2. **What do you do?** — Category definition (be specific)
3. **What's your vision?** — Where is the category going?
4. **What wave are you riding?** — Macro trend alignment
5. **Who shares the space?** — Competitive landscape mapping
6. **What makes you the "only"?** — Onliness statement
7. **What should you add or subtract?** — Focus (subtract > add)

### Phase 2: Design Your Zag
8. **Who loves you?** — Brand tribe / community definition
9. **How do you explain yourself?** — Trueline (authentic tagline)
10. **How do you spread the word?** — Touchpoint strategy
11. **How do you earn loyalty?** — Experience design

### Phase 3: Renew Your Zag
12. **Are you building a portfolio?** — Brand architecture
13. **Are you protecting your brand?** — Trademark, consistency
14. **When should you re-zag?** — Category maturity signals

### Competitive Positioning Map
- Plot competitors on 2 key dimensions (from customer perspective)
- Find white space where NO competitor operates
- Position your brand in the white space
- Validate: is the white space empty because nobody wants it, or because nobody's tried?

## Marty Neumeier: Brand Flip

### The Old Model (Company-Centric)
- Company defines brand → pushes to customers
- Brand = what WE say we are
- Customers are targets

### The New Model (Customer-Centric)
- Customers define brand → company serves the tribe
- Brand = what THEY say we are
- Customers are tribe members

### Brand Flip Principles
1. **From identity to identification** — Customers don't buy brands, they join them
2. **From trust to delight** — Trust is table stakes; aim for tribal belonging
3. **From features to meaning** — Features inform; meaning transforms
4. **From static to dynamic** — Brand evolves with its community
5. **From authority to authenticity** — In AI era: transparency about AI use IS authenticity

## AI-Era Brand Extensions

### LLM Voice Card
Define how AI agents representing this brand should communicate:

```
Brand: [Name]
Core Voice: [3-5 adjective descriptors]
Tone Range: [formal ←→ casual spectrum position]
Vocabulary Level: [technical ←→ accessible]
Humor: [never / rarely / occasionally / frequently]
Emoji Use: [never / minimal / moderate]
Response Length Preference: [concise / balanced / detailed]
Forbidden Words: [list]
Signature Phrases: [list]
```

### Agent Personality Traits
- **Proactivity:** Does the agent offer suggestions or wait to be asked?
- **Formality:** First name basis or formal?
- **Expertise Display:** Does the agent show its reasoning or just give answers?
- **Empathy Level:** How much does the agent acknowledge emotions?
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Localization and cultural adaptation rules

### AI Transparency Guidelines
- When must the brand disclose AI involvement?
- How does the brand frame AI as a feature vs. a tool?
- What's the brand's stance on AI-generated vs. human-created content?

## Onliness Statement Template

```
Our [OFFERING] is the only [CATEGORY] that [DIFFERENTIATOR]
for [AUDIENCE] who [NEED/JOB]
unlike [COMPETITORS] who [CONVENTIONAL APPROACH]
we [UNIQUE MECHANISM] which means [ULTIMATE BENEFIT].
```

Fill every blank. If you can't fill [DIFFERENTIATOR] compellingly, the brand needs more work.
