# Brand Strategy Frameworks

## Marty Neumeier: Brand Gap (5 Disciplines)

**Core thesis:** "A brand is a person's gut feeling about a product, service, or company." The brand gap is the distance between business strategy and customer experience. A **charismatic brand** is one for which people believe there is no substitute — claiming 50%+ market share and up to 40% price premiums.

### 1. Differentiate
Three questions every brand must answer: **Who are you? What do you do? Why does it matter?**
- **The Onliness Test:** "Our [offering] is the only [category] that [differentiator]"
- If you can't complete this sentence compellingly, you don't have a brand
- Differentiation = different, not better. Occupy #1 in a niche rather than competing broadly
- Higher pricing reduces commoditization risk

### 2. Collaborate
- Brand building requires cross-functional alignment
- In AI era: alignment between human team AND AI agents
- Brand voice must be consistent whether human or AI speaks
- Design system = brand's shared language

### 3. Innovate
- Seek **MAYA** — Most Advanced Yet Acceptable
- Innovation is brand fuel — brands that stop innovating die
- Test: Would removing this feature/trait make customers care?
- AI-era: AI capabilities ARE brand differentiators (speed, personalization, availability)

**Seven Criteria for Brand Naming:**

| # | Criterion | Test |
|---|-----------|------|
| 1 | Distinctiveness | Stands out in speech stream? |
| 2 | Brevity | 4 syllables or less? |
| 3 | Appropriateness | Fits business without sounding generic? |
| 4 | Easy spelling/pronunciation | Searchable without guessing? |
| 5 | Likability | Feels and sounds good? |
| 6 | Extendability | Can be played with creatively? |
| 7 | Protectability | Trademarkable, domain available? |

### 4. Validate
Four brand testing methods:

| Test | Method | Measures |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Swap Test** | Replace your name/visual with competitor's | If it still works, not differentiated enough |
| **Hand Test** | Cover the trademark | If you can't tell who's talking, voice isn't distinctive |
| **Concept Test** | Show to ~10 audience members, qualitative | Right idea (neighborhood) then right execution (address) |
| **Field Test** | Deploy prototype in real sales environment | Distinctiveness, relevance, memorability, extendability, depth |

### 5. Cultivate
- Brand is a living system, not a document
- Brand guidelines must be actionable (tokens + voice guide), not aspirational
- Every touchpoint reinforces or erodes brand
- AI era: every AI interaction IS a brand touchpoint

## Marty Neumeier: Zag (17-Step Process)

"When everybody zigs, zag." Radical differentiation means finding a whole new market space you can own.

### Phase 1: Find Your Zag
1. **Who are you?** — Core identity, mission, values
2. **What do you do?** — Category definition (be specific)
3. **What's your vision?** — Where is the category going?
4. **What wave are you riding?** — Macro trend alignment
5. **Who shares the space?** — Competitive landscape mapping
6. **What makes you the "only"?** — Onliness statement
7. **What should you add or subtract?** — Focus (subtract > add)

### Phase 2: Design Your Zag
8. **Who loves you?** — Brand tribe / community definition
9. **Who is the enemy?** — Primary competitor identification
10. **What do they call you?** — Brand perception
11. **How do you explain yourself?** — Trueline (authentic tagline)
12. **How do you spread the word?** — Communication strategy
13. **How do people engage with you?** — Customer interactions

### Phase 3: Sustain Your Zag
14. **What do they experience?** — Customer experience design
15. **How do you earn their loyalty?** — Retention mechanisms
16. **How do you extend your success?** — Growth planning
17. **How do you protect your portfolio?** — Long-term sustainability

### Competitive Positioning Map
- Plot competitors on 2 key dimensions (from customer perspective)
- Find white space where NO competitor operates
- Position your brand in the white space
- Validate: is the white space empty because nobody wants it, or because nobody's tried?

## Marty Neumeier: Brand Flip (18 Flips)

**Core thesis:** Customers now run brands. Power shifted from company-driven to customer-driven.

### The 18 Brand Flips

|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Products | Meaning |

### Brand Commitment Matrix

Two-column alignment framework — both sides must align for brand coherence:

| Customer Side (IAM) | Company Side (POV) | Alignment Check |
|---------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **I**dentity — Who they are | **P**urpose — Why we exist | Purpose serves their identity? |
| **A**ims — What they want | **O**nlyness — What we uniquely offer | Onlyness fulfills their aims? |
| **M**ores — Their tribal values | **V**alues — How we behave | Values match their mores? |

### Brand Commitment Scale (Brand Ladder)

Score via 8-statement survey (2 per rung, rated 1-5):

| Rung | Level | Description | Multiplier | Max |
|------|-------|-------------|------------|-----|
| 1 | Satisfaction | Product met expectations, trust begins | x1 | 10 |
| 2 | Delight | Surprise beyond baseline, sparks emotion | x2 | 20 |
| 3 | Engagement | Joins brand tribe, increasing loyalty | x3 | 30 |
| 4 | Empowerment | Depends on brand for status/growth, becomes advocate | x4 | 40 |

**Total possible: 100.** Run at regular intervals to track brand equity.

## Marty Neumeier: Scramble (Agile Brand Strategy)

Compress traditional 6-18 month strategy into < 6 weeks.

### The 5 Qs of Strategy

| Q | Question | Focus |
|---|----------|-------|
| 1 | What is our purpose? | Starting place for all strategy |
| 2 | Who do we serve? | Customer identification |
| 3 | Where should we compete? | Category choice (key to winning) |
| 4 | How will we win? | Positioning via Onlyness formula |
| 5 | How will we grow? | Culture built around purpose |

### The 5 Ps of Design Thinking

| P | Step | Method |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | Problemizing | Reframe problems — is this the right problem? |
| 2 | Pinballing | Generate multiple courses by bouncing ideas off obstacles |
| 3 | Probing | Edward de Bono's Six Thinking Hats for parallel thinking |
| 4 | Prototyping | Insert "making" between knowing and doing |
| 5 | Proofing | Test assumptions against marketplace reality |

## AI-Era Brand Extensions

### LLM Voice Card
Define how AI agents representing this brand should communicate:

```
Brand: [Name]
Core Voice: [3-5 adjective descriptors]
Tone Range: [formal <-> casual spectrum position]
Vocabulary Level: [technical <-> accessible]
Humor: [never / rarely / occasionally / frequently]
Emoji Use: [never / minimal / moderate]
Response Length Preference: [concise / balanced / detailed]
Forbidden Words: [list]
Signature Phrases: [list]
Brand Archetype: [Helper / Expert / Friend / Guide]
```

### Agent Personality Traits
- **Proactivity:** Does the agent offer suggestions or wait to be asked?
- **Formality:** First name basis or formal?
- **Expertise Display:** Does the agent show its reasoning or just give answers?
- **Empathy Level:** How much does the agent acknowledge emotions?
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Localization and cultural adaptation rules

### AI-Native Brand Assets (beyond traditional)

| Asset | Format | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| LLM Voice Card | Structured text/JSON | Translate brand voice into LLM parameters |
| Prompt Style Guide | 20-30 reusable prompts | Team consistency across AI tools |
| Conversational Guidelines | Tone spectrum + do/don't | Define agent interaction patterns |
| Brand Voice Examples | 10-15 on-brand samples + anti-examples | Quality reference library |
| System Prompt Templates | Foundational LLM instructions | Personality, knowledge boundaries, escalation |
| llms.txt | Text file at root | Make brand info AI-accessible (like robots.txt) |

### Tone Matrix Dimensions

| Dimension | Spectrum |
|-----------|----------|
| Professional | <-> Conversational |
| Educational | <-> Entertaining |
| Direct | <-> Nuanced |
| Authoritative | <-> Collaborative |
| Assertive | <-> Patient |
| Humorous | <-> Serious |
| Empathetic | <-> Solutions-focused |

### AI Transparency Guidelines
- When must the brand disclose AI involvement?
- How does the brand frame AI as a feature vs. a tool?
- What's the brand's stance on AI-generated vs. human-created content?
- "Share of Model" metric: monitor how LLMs describe your brand vs competitors

### Brand as Code Principles
- If AI builds your product, docs, UI, and marketing, your brand must be legible to AI
- Encode brand rules in machine-readable formats (JSON, not PDF)
- Voice is no longer human-to-human — it's human-to-machine-to-human
- For each personality trait, write 3-5 specific rules that operationalize it

## Onliness Statement Template

```
Our [OFFERING] is the only [CATEGORY] that [DIFFERENTIATOR]
for [AUDIENCE] who [NEED/JOB]
unlike [COMPETITORS] who [CONVENTIONAL APPROACH]
we [UNIQUE MECHANISM] which means [ULTIMATE BENEFIT].
```

Fill every blank. If you can't fill [DIFFERENTIATOR] compellingly, the brand needs more work.

**Differentiation Test:** Replace your brand name with a competitor's. If the statement still works, you need more differentiation.
